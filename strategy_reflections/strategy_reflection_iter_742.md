# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 742 Report

Although strategy_v800 introduced a suite of theoretically motivated upgrades—pT‑scaled dynamic σW and σT, a Student‑t top‑mass term, a log‑sum‑exp over the three dijet masses, a tanh‑shaped asymmetry damping at low pT, and a lightweight multi‑stage veto—the observed efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 fell short of the 65 % goal and even under‑performed the prior 63.8 % baseline, suggesting that the added sophistication over‑regularized the signal: the dynamic resolution, while intended to rescue low‑pT tops, broadened the acceptance so much that the downstream selector tightened its threshold to control background, the ν≈4 Student‑t tail penalised genuine top‑mass peaks that were closer to Gaussian than anticipated, the smooth log‑sum‑exp diluted the discriminating power of the best W‑candidate by flattening the likelihood landscape, the tanh‑damping turned off a useful asymmetry handle even for moderate‑pT tops, and the deterministic veto (e.g., the hard pt > 30 GeV cut and strict dijet‑mass caps) inadvertently culled a non‑negligible fraction of real low‑pT triplets the dynamic terms were meant to keep; to move forward we should re‑tune the σ scaling functions using data‑driven resolution estimates, perform a systematic scan of the Student‑t ν parameter (including values approaching a Gaussian), optimise the steepness and break‑point of the tanh asymmetry factor, replace the hard pt floor with a probabilistic soft cut, experiment with alternative smoothing kernels (e.g., a temperature‑controlled soft‑max) that preserve a sharper distinction among W‑pairings, relax or adapt the veto thresholds based on validation‑sample studies, and finally augment the hand‑crafted likelihood with ancillary discriminants such as b‑tag scores or jet‑substructure observables fed into a simple multivariate classifier (e.g., a BDT) that can learn the optimal combination of all terms, while continuously monitoring ROC curves on independent samples to guarantee that the revised model genuinely exceeds the 65 % efficiency target without inflating the fake‑rate.
