# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 750 Report

The “strategy_v750” harnessed orthogonal physics cues and a pT‑dependent gating to temper the high‑pT‑tuned BDT where resolution degrades, while a hard 30 GeV W‑mass veto stripped away the longest QCD tails, delivering a reconstruction efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 – a noticeable gain over the baseline but still shy of the 64 % target; the shortfall likely arises from the binary veto discarding a non‑trivial fraction of genuine signal events whose dijet masses wander beyond the cut due to jet‑energy smearing, and from the sigmoid gate possibly under‑weighting the BDT in the intermediate‑pT region where its discrimination remains valuable, suggesting the next step is to replace the hard veto with a soft penalty or adaptive mass window, retune or replace the pT‑gate (or train separate classifiers for low‑ and high‑pT slices), systematically optimise the fractional exponents, and enrich the discriminant with additional observables such as b‑tagging scores, jet‑substructure variables, and angular correlations, all calibrated on data control regions to ensure robust performance across the full kinematic spectrum.
