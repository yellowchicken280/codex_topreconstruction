# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 707 Report

The modest gain of ≈2 % in signal efficiency (0.616 ± 0.015) shows that the soft‑veto, pT‑aware formulation is indeed helping to rescue some genuine boosted‑top candidates that were lost to the hard 2.5σ window, but the weight balance and penalty shapes were not optimal enough to reach the ≈65 % goal—likely because the reduced exponent on the raw BDT (0.45) over‑flattens its contribution, the logistic mapping compresses the dynamic range of the BDT scores, and the chosen Student‑t degrees of freedom and σ(pT) scaling still leave too much weight on outliers or under‑penalise high‑pT mass mismatches; additionally, the arctan(pT) factor (exponent 0.05) may be too weak to fully compensate the worsening jet‑energy resolution at multi‑TeV scales. Going forward, we should re‑tune the exponent hierarchy (perhaps restoring a slightly stronger BDT term, e.g. 0.55–0.60), adjust the Student‑t ν parameter and σ(pT) functional form using an explicit profile of resolution versus pT, experiment with alternative bounded mappings (e.g. a calibrated sigmoid or Platt scaling) that preserve discriminative power, and consider a pT‑dependent weight for the mass terms so that high‑pT triplets receive proportionally larger penalties for large deviations. Finally, augment the input feature set with sub‑structure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions) and retrain the underlying BDT (or switch to a more expressive model such as XGBoost or a shallow neural net) to capture any residual correlations that the current linear‑combination framework cannot model, while continuously monitoring the fake‑rate to ensure that any efficiency gains do not come at the cost of increased background contamination.
