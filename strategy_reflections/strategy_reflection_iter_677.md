# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 677 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 demonstrates that the pT‑adaptive σ‑scaling and the soft‑max combination of the three dijet Gaussian terms indeed opened up the dynamic range for highly boosted tops, but the penalty ingredients we introduced over‑constrained the signal, especially in the intermediate‑pT region where the natural spread of the W‑ and top‑mass peaks is still sizeable; the linear growth of σ may have become too generous for the highest‑pT candidates, letting background creep in, while the asymmetry exponential (≈30 GeV scale) and the hard veto (3σ cut) appear to be too aggressive on legitimate radiation‑induced mass shifts, shaving off genuine tops and leaving the overall efficiency just shy of the 0.64 goal. The next step should be a systematic re‑tuning of these non‑linear pieces: replace the linear σ(pT) with a sub‑linear (e.g. σ ∝ √pT) or piecewise function that respects the empirical mass resolution per pT bin, relax the asymmetry scale (perhaps via a data‑driven calibration) and soften the hard veto to 2.5σ; concurrently explore alternative aggregations such as a log‑sum‑exp “soft‑max” that still rewards at least one W‑like pair but penalises outliers less harshly, and fine‑tune the BDT sigmoid centre and power‑law stretch using cross‑validated hyper‑parameter scans. Adding complementary substructure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy correlation functions) to the BDT could also recover lost signal, while a per‑pT bin optimisation of the final 0.95 power will let us amplify the tail where the signal‑background separation is strongest without sacrificing overall efficiency.
