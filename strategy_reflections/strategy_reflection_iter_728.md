# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 728 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 shows that the pT‑aware refinements did recover some of the semi‑boosted tops that were previously lost—particularly the softer σₘ at low pT and the rescue flag that rescued a handful of borderline candidates—but the overall veto remained too aggressive, mainly because the asymmetry penalty still ramps up too quickly and the 0.65 BDT exponent does not compress enough of the low‑score tail to let the rescue signal dominate; consequently many genuine low‑pT tops are still being discarded and the multiplicative combination limits our ability to rebalance the relative importance of each term. To push the efficiency above the 0.64 goal we should (i) re‑tune the asymmetry scaling (e.g. replace \(1‑e^{-p_T/400}\) with a gentler turn‑on or add a floor at low pT), (ii) explore a slightly stronger non‑linear BDT compression (exponent ≈ 0.55) so the rescue flag has more leverage, (iii) broaden the rescue criteria (perhaps requiring both W‑mass pulls < 1.5σ and a relaxed asymmetry cut) and test a small additive term rather than a pure multiplier, and (iv) perform a systematic hyper‑parameter scan on a validation set that isolates the semi‑boosted regime, possibly adding a dedicated low‑pT sub‑classifier or extra sub‑structure variables to compensate for the residual over‑pruning. These targeted adjustments should preserve the background rejection achieved by the baseline while finally breaking the 0.64 efficiency barrier.
