# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 687 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % ± 1.5 % shows that the physics‑driven “quality score’’ succeeded in making the discriminant more robust in the boosted regime—thanks to the pT‑dependent σ, the triple‑product W weight, and the soft pT‑boost—but the aggressive low‑pT vetoes and the strong variance and mass‑balance penalties still cost too many true tops, leaving us short of the 64 % goal; in particular, the linear 0.001 pT scaling and the 0.55 exponent on the raw BDT overly suppress candidates where the raw BDT is already informative, while the mass‑balance denominator may be too stringent for intermediate pT where resolution is only modestly worse. The next step is to retune these hyper‑parameters: experiment with a milder mass‑balance penalty (e.g. a pT‑dependent power law rather than a simple exponential), shift the sigmoid turn‑on lower (≈250 GeV) to rescue midspectrum tops, increase the linear boost factor or replace it with a pT‑squared term, and raise the BDT exponent (≈0.65) to preserve its dynamic range; simultaneous grid‑search or Bayesian optimization on a validation set, possibly complemented by a lightweight neural‑net that learns optimal weights for the physics terms, should reveal whether a more balanced combination can finally push the efficiency past the 64 % barrier without sacrificing background rejection.
