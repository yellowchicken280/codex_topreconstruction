# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 695 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the Student‑t tail and the pₜ‑dependent σ indeed softened the low‑pₜ penalty and kept the background under control, but the aggressive spread‑penalty, the soft veto at 130 GeV, and the tanh‑compressed BDT term together removed too many legitimate top candidates—especially in the intermediate‑pₜ region where the adaptive boost (0.6 vs 0.25) does not fully compensate—so the score still falls short of the 0.64 goal; to push the efficiency past the barrier we should re‑tune the Student‑t degrees of freedom and the σ(pₜ) scaling to allow a slightly broader core, replace the hard spread‑penalty with a softer, perhaps learned weighting, raise the soft‑veto threshold or make it pₜ‑dependent, explore a smoother transition for the boost exponent (e.g. a continuous function rather than a step at 250 GeV) and adjust the non‑linear exponents, and finally run a systematic hyper‑parameter optimisation (grid or Bayesian) on a validation set, possibly adding a lightweight neural‑network combiner to capture correlations among the three W‑mass Gaussians—these refinements should increase signal acceptance while preserving the observed background rejection.
