# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 678 Report

The ≈ 62 % efficiency achieved by strategy B67 shows that the physics‑driven refinements—replacing the linear σ(pT) with a √pT scaling that mirrors the true calorimeter resolution, building a χ²‑like mass‑likelihood that adapts to the degrading resolution at high pT, and introducing a soft logistic pT prior—successfully rescued many tops that were previously vetoed by an overly aggressive 3σ cut while still curbing the most egregious background configurations through the generous χ²≤25 gate and per‑pair 2.5 σ outlier test; the non‑linear exponent weighting allowed the mass‑likelihood to dominate only when its precision was high, preserving the BDT’s discriminating power in ambiguous regions. Nonetheless, the modest gain suggests the remaining leakage at the extreme high‑pT tail and the residual inefficiency for tops with asymmetric radiation still limit performance, likely because the σ(pT) model, though sub‑linear, may still under‑estimate resolution fluctuations and the fixed χ² thresholds do not adapt to event‑by‑event variations. The next step should be a data‑driven calibration of the σ(pT) function (e.g. using Z→jj or tt̄ control samples) and an exploration of dynamic χ² or outlier thresholds that scale with the per‑event covariance matrix; simultaneously, augmenting the discriminant with complementary substructure variables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions) or replacing the static exponent weighting with a shallow neural‑network combiner could further lift efficiency while keeping the background under control.
