# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 733 Report

The new strategy delivered a modest gain – the overall top‑tag efficiency rose to 0.616 ± 0.015 (≈1.5 σ below the 0.64–0.65 goal) – because the softened spread veto preserved many semi‑boosted candidates, the lowered‑centre, steeper logistic transform rescued signal in the 0.15–0.25 BDT window, and the pₜ‑dependent exponent together with the tanh boost gave a measurable uplift for the most energetic tops; however, the gain was insufficient to reach the target: the tanh term only added ≤20 % at very high pₜ, the exponent’s growth (0.8→1.2) proved too gentle to fully counteract the residual high‑pₜ suppression, and the fractional exponents (0.7, 0.6, 0.5) diluted the impact of the mass‑pull and W‑mass reward terms, while the logistic spread step still discards a tail of legitimate high‑spread topologies.  To close the remaining gap we should (i) steepen the pₜ‑scaling of the exponent (or introduce a piecewise‑linear boost that ramps more aggressively above pₜ≈250 GeV), (ii) re‑tune the logistic spread parameters (lower the threshold and narrow the width) via a dedicated cross‑validation scan, (iii) explore a milder fractional exponent for the mass‑pull (e.g. 0.8) so the Gaussian reward retains more weight at high boost, (iv) incorporate additional substructure discriminants such as N‑subjettiness or a shallow CNN‑based tagger as an auxiliary input, and (v) perform a Bayesian hyper‑parameter optimisation across the full set of transforms to locate the global optimum.  Implementing these refinements and re‑evaluating the efficiency in pₜ‑binned slices will reveal whether we can consistently push the reconstruction efficiency past the 0.64 threshold while maintaining background rejection.
