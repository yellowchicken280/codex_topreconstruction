# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 627 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % shows that the physics‑driven “best‑pair’’ weighting and the softened pₜ–dependent tanh scaling indeed rescued many tops that were previously down‑weighted at moderate boosts – the balanced combination of the two W‑mass deviations, the Gaussian top‑mass term and the sum‑rule constraint sharpened the signal peak without over‑penalising occasional jet‑energy fluctuations – but the hard low‑pₜ veto, the fixed Gaussian width on the top mass, and the rather strict balance term together still trim away a non‑negligible fraction of correctly reconstructed candidates, especially when resolution smearing or extra radiation skews one of the dijet masses just enough to fail the veto; the current fixed exponents in the weighted geometric mean also limit the flexibility of the classifier to adapt to differing background shapes. The next step should therefore be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (varying the Gaussian σ, the balance‑term tolerance, the veto threshold, and the relative weights in the geometric mean) possibly replacing the hard veto with a smooth penalty, and exploring more expressive multivariate combos (e.g. a shallow neural net or BDT) that can learn optimal nonlinear couplings of the same physics variables while preserving decorrelation with the top mass; additionally, incorporating auxiliary observables such as b‑tag scores, N‑subjettiness or SoftDrop mass, and calibrating the tanh scaling on data‑driven pₜ‑dependent resolutions should help push the efficiency past the 65 % target without sacrificing background rejection.
