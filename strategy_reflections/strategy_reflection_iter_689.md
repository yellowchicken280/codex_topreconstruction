# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 689 Report

The 61.6 % efficiency (±1.5 %) indicates that while the new χ²‑based likelihood and smoother pₜ weighting helped tame combinatorial background, they also introduced a few overly aggressive cuts that trimmed genuine tops: the early broad‑mass veto appears to be discarding a non‑negligible fraction of signal, and the mass‑balance term—intended to penalise kinematically implausible W‑pair spreads—seems to be too punitive for realistic variations in the W‑mass distribution, especially at moderate boosts where the three‑jet system naturally exhibits a larger spread; additionally, the logistic pₜ weight, though gentler than the previous quadratic boost, may not be rising steeply enough in the 300–500 GeV regime that historically drove the efficiency gap, and the sub‑linear exponent on the raw BDT score, while preserving borderline candidates, may be diluting the discriminating power of the BDT and letting background linger, prompting the χ² stage to down‑weight many true tops. To close the gap to the 64 % target, the next iteration should (i) relax or re‑tune the early veto window (perhaps moving it closer to the true top‑mass peak and shaping it with a data‑driven sideband), (ii) recalibrate the mass‑balance weight—e.g. by introducing a modest Gaussian penalty rather than a hard cut—and (iii) explore steeper logistic parameters or a piecewise‑linear pₜ boost that more aggressively rewards the 300–500 GeV band while preserving the high‑pₜ tail; finally, a modest increase of the BDT exponent (or an additional post‑BDT multivariate layer) could restore some background rejection without sacrificing signal, and a focused hyper‑parameter scan across these knobs, validated on a dedicated control sample, will pinpoint the optimal balance between purity and efficiency.
