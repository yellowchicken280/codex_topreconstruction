# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 682 Report

The new smooth‑weighting scheme did succeed in softening the brutal vetoes that previously crippled moderate‑fluctuation top candidates— the logistic‐scaled BDT, the Gaussian top‑mass pull and the gentle pT uplift all raise the baseline score for realistic signal‑like events—yet the achieved true‑top efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 still falls short of the 0.64 benchmark, suggesting that the current parametrisation still over‑penalises a sizable fraction of genuine tops. The most plausible reasons are that the top‑mass Gaussian (σ≈12 GeV) and the 15 % relative width for the best‑W‑mass pull are still too tight relative to the detector resolution, so tails of the mass distribution are unduly down‑weighted; the additive combination lets the spread‑penalty dominate for events with larger dijet‑mass spreads, and the logistic mapping may compress the high‑BDT tail, preventing the best candidates from reaching sufficiently high final scores. The logical next step is a systematic scan of these hyper‑parameters—e.g. widening the top‑mass σ to ~15 GeV, loosening the W‑mass pull, and reducing the weight of the spread Gaussian—or moving to a partially multiplicative formulation for the spread term; an ablation study to quantify each component’s effect, followed by retraining the BDT with an enriched feature set (including angular and sub‑structure variables) and cross‑validation to guard against over‑fitting, should reveal whether a more nuanced weighting can finally push the efficiency above 64 % while preserving competitive background rejection.
