# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 721 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 in signal efficiency shows that the smoother, pT‑dependent pieces of typeB_strategy_v1_v721 succeeded in rescuing high‑pT tops that were previously over‑penalised by the hard‑tanh boost and fixed‑width asymmetry term—thanks to the sigmoid weight, the soft‑max combination of the three dijet‑mass Gaussian penalties, and the logarithmically widening asymmetry width, which together preserve more genuine boosted‑top kinematics while still down‑weighting low‑pT backgrounds; however, the overall performance is still constrained by a residual loss of borderline events (the quadratic soft veto still suppresses a non‑trivial fraction of signal) and by the fixed shape of the Gaussian top‑mass term, which may not optimally capture the broader mass distribution at very high pT. The next step should be a systematic scan of the sigmoid steepness and offset (e.g. varying the pT scale and slope), a re‑tuning of the Gaussian width for the top‑mass term (perhaps making it pT‑dependent as well), and exploring a more flexible soft‑veto (e.g. a piecewise polynomial or learned weighting) to recover those borderline cases; additionally, incorporating complementary observables such as N‑subjettiness or energy‑correlation functions and validating the tuned parameters on an independent validation set will help determine whether the observed efficiency improvement is robust and whether further gains can be harvested without inflating background mistag rates.
