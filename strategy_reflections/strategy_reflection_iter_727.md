# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 727 Report

The new multi‑stage classifier certainly sharpened the decision surface and reduced background leakage, but the very same ingredients that tightened the mass likelihood and penalised extreme dijet‑mass asymmetries also over‑pruned genuine semi‑boosted tops: the early veto (low BDT + large top‑mass pull) removed a non‑trivial fraction of signal events where one of the three jets is naturally soft, while the aggressive shape‑variable penalty and the p_T‑dependent resolution floors squeezed the mass term too much at low p_T, and the tanh(p_T/380) damping squelched high‑p_T tails where real tops still populate. Consequently the overall efficiency settled at 0.616 ± 0.015, short of the ≈ 0.65 target. The next step should be a systematic retuning of these knobs – relax the early‑veto threshold, soften the asymmetry penalty (or make it p_T‑dependent), lower the exponent on the mass‑likelihood toward unity, and recalibrate the resolution floor using data‑driven jet‑energy‑resolution studies. A small hyper‑parameter scan (or a meta‑learner such as a shallow neural net) that jointly optimises the exponent, tanh scaling, and veto cut can then be applied to recover the lost signal while preserving the improved background rejection, thereby pushing the working point toward the desired efficiency frontier.
