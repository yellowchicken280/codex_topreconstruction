# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 603 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency of strategy_B_v603 shows that the adaptive ingredients—‑the 1/√pₜ‐scaled Gaussian kernels, the multi‑stage veto, the light‑tailed Student‑t penalty (ν≈1.7), the mass‑asymmetry score and the low‑pₜ‑boosting exponent—indeed rescued a sizable fraction of soft tops that would otherwise have been discarded, confirming the core hypothesis that resolution‑driven smearing can be mitigated by pₜ‑dependent smoothing and a gentler tail penalty; however, the overall gain fell short of the 64 % plateau, suggesting that the rescue mechanisms were either not aggressive enough (e.g. ν may still be too low, still over‑penalising large mass excursions) or were counter‑balanced by an overly stringent veto and exponent that down‑weights noisy low‑pₜ candidates and attenuates high‑pₜ signal. The next iteration should therefore fine‑tune the heavy‑tail parameter (perhaps exploring ν∈[1.2,2.0] or a mixture of Gaussian‑Student‑t components), reshape the pₜ‑dependent exponent curve (e.g. a smoother transition rather than a hard 0.55 floor at pₜ<30 GeV), and introduce complementary discriminants such as b‑tag probabilities or jet‑substructure variables to bolster the mass‑asymmetry requirement without sacrificing genuine tops. A systematic hyper‑parameter scan (grid or Bayesian optimisation) together with cross‑validation on independent signal/background samples will clarify which adjustments provide the best trade‑off between rescuing low‑pₜ tops and preserving background rejection, guiding the strategy toward surpassing the previous efficiency ceiling.
