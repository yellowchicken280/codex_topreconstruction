# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 717 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.0152 efficiency shows that, despite the appealing physics‑motivated smoothness of strategy_typeB_v65, the net effect was a loss of signal rather than the hoped‑for 1 % gain needed to breach the 64 % barrier – the result sits more than three standard deviations below the target, indicating that the new ingredients have over‑constrained the selection; in particular the pT‑dependent top‑mass chi‑square together with the dynamic mass window and the three sequential vetoes become too aggressive for the boosted regime, wiping out many genuine high‑pT top candidates, while the softened BDT exponent (0.85) dilutes the weight of the otherwise most discriminating triplets and the quartic asymmetry term, though milder, still suppresses asymmetric decays that are characteristic of boosted tops. The smooth sigmoid/tanh turn‑on does preserve low‑pT candidates but does not compensate for the additional signal loss elsewhere, and the background level remains unchanged, so the trade‑off is unfavorable. The next step should be a systematic ablation study to isolate the impact of each component, followed by a targeted retuning: relax the dynamic top‑mass window at high pT (perhaps using a pT‑scaled σ instead of a fixed cut), explore a milder BDT softening (exponent ≈0.95) or even revert to the raw score for the top‑score region, and test lower asymmetry exponents (e.g. (asym‑1)^2) to recover genuine asymmetric decays. Incorporating additional boosted‑top observables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions) or moving to a multivariate learner that can automatically balance these continuous weights may also recover the missing efficiency while keeping the background under control.
