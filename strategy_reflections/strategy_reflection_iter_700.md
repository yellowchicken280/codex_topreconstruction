# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 700 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the smoother, more continuous ingredients of strategy_v701 did recover a slice of the signal that the hard Gaussian cuts were discarding—especially the pₜ‑dependent logistic boost, which lifted many mid‑range tops, and the broadened σ values that eased the detector‑level smearing—but the overall efficiency still falls short of the 0.64 target because the asymmetry penalty and the final >2σ W‑mass veto remain too punitive, effectively re‑imposing a binary loss on a non‑negligible fraction of otherwise acceptable candidates and diluting the benefit of the extra weight given to the raw BDT and top‑mass likelihood. To push past the barrier we should revisit the exponentiation scheme (e.g. dialing the top‑mass term up to ∼35 % and reducing the asymmetry exponent), refine the asymmetry‑factor shape (perhaps using a gentler, quartic‑instead‑of‑quadratic penalty) and test slightly narrower σ values (≈12–13 GeV for the top) that balance resolution against background leakage; simultaneous hyper‑parameter optimisation (via Bayesian or grid searches) of the logistic‑boost steepness and the sigmoid remapping scaling could also reveal a more optimal trade‑off. Adding complementary discriminants—such as jet substructure variables, b‑tag scores, or a deep‑learning meta‑classifier that learns the optimal non‑linear combination—would give the algorithm extra leverage, while a thorough cross‑validation on independent signal and background samples will guard against over‑tuning and confirm that any efficiency uplift is robust.
