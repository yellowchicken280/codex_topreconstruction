# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 694 Report

The modest 61.6 % signal efficiency (≈ 1.5 % below the 64 % goal) tells us that the physics‑driven ingredients of strategy_v720 are largely on the right track— the pₜ‑dependent Gaussian mass likelihoods and the χ² combination of the three dijet masses do sharpen the topology selection, and the sub‑linear BDT damping together with the tanh‑scaled boost prevent a few extreme scores from dominating the ranking; however, the current parametrisation over‑penalises marginally inconsistent events, especially at low pₜ where the Gaussian widths may be too narrow and the logistic spread term too aggressive, while the chosen exponents in the geometric mean give insufficient weight to the high‑pₜ boost that could recover more genuine tops. To push the efficiency past the target we should first re‑calibrate the pₜ‑dependent σ’s using data‑driven resolution studies, explore a softer spread penalty (e.g. a piecewise‑linear or student‑t likelihood) and modestly increase the exponent on the tanh boost, and then re‑optimize the geometric‑mean exponents via a grid search or Bayesian optimisation on a validation set that explicitly balances signal efficiency against background rejection. Adding complementary discriminants—such as b‑tag scores, ΔR separations of the jet pairs, and jet‑substructure observables—into the BDT (or switching to a deeper gradient‑boosted ensemble) can also capture residual correlations that the current χ² term misses. Finally, systematic cross‑validation and an explicit study of the efficiency‑background trade‑off will guide whether a slightly tighter cut on the composite score yields a net gain in the overall analysis significance, informing the next iteration of the strategy.
