# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 711 Report

The modest but robust efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 for strategy_topBoostV4 can be traced to three core design choices: the replacement of rigid 2σ/2.5σ windows with Gaussian likelihoods let partially‑mismeasured candidates survive, preserving genuine W‑boson and top‑mass peaks that would otherwise be cut away; the cubic‑power‑law asymmetry penalty and the dijet‑mass‑balance factor together acted as a very effective QCD‑rejector by down‑weighting the characteristic mass‑imbalance of three‑jet backgrounds while rewarding the symmetric mass topology of true W→qq′ decays; and the pT‑dependent sigmoid, together with a sigmoid‑normalised BDT output that is multiplied by the likelihood terms using tuned exponents, amplified strong signal features without letting an isolated background fluctuation dominate the score. The final logistic squashing gave a well‑behaved discriminator that could be cut on with a single threshold. Nonetheless, the approach also exposed a few weaknesses: the hard low‑pT veto discards a non‑trivial tail of signal, the multiplicative combination can over‑suppress events where one component (e.g. the BDT) is modestly supportive, and the fixed Gaussian widths and cubic penalty exponent assume a static background shape that may not hold across the full phase space. To push the performance higher, the next iteration should (i) relax or re‑shape the low‑pT veto (perhaps using a pT‑dependent penalty rather than a hard cut), (ii) replace the hand‑crafted exponents with a small neural‑network or a calibrated gradient‑boosted regressor that learns the optimal non‑linear combination of the likelihoods and BDT score, (iii) allow the Gaussian widths and the asymmetry‑penalty power to vary with jet kinematics (e.g. via conditional parametrisation or bin‑wise fits), and (iv) enrich the feature set with sub‑structure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions, soft‑drop mass) that capture residual correlations the current factorised likelihoods miss. A systematic hyper‑parameter scan on an independent validation sample, coupled with a data‑driven background calibration, will then reveal whether these refinements translate the modest gain in efficiency into a statistically significant improvement in signal significance.
