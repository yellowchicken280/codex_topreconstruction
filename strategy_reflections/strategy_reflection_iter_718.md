# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 718 Report

The modest 0.9 % lift to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the new ingredients – a pt‑dependent top‑mass σ, a W‑mass χ² term, a softened BDT exponent and a multi‑stage veto – succeeded in rescuing a few of the most extreme boosted candidates but the overall selection remains too restrictive to breach the 0.65 goal; the pt‑scaling of the mass resolutions is still too gentle, the χ² penalties (mass_chi2 > 9, w_chi2_sum > 12) and the asymmetry cut (asym > 0.45) continue to veto a sizable fraction of legitimate high‑pt tops, while the generic tanh‑sigmoid combination with fixed coefficients (a≈1.2, b≈0.6, c≈0.3, d≈2.0, e≈1.5) was not re‑optimised for the new penalty landscape, and the sub‑linear BDT exponent, though smoothing out outliers, also dilutes the discriminating power on the signal‑rich tail.  Going forward the most effective route will be a systematic re‑tuning: perform a hyper‑parameter scan (or Bayesian optimisation) of the χ² thresholds, asymmetry limit and combination weights, explore a steeper pt‑dependence for σ (e.g. σ = 10 + 0.05·pt) or even a full kinematic fit that replaces simple χ² penalties, train separate BDTs (or deep‑learning taggers) for low‑pt and high‑pt regimes to preserve the sharpness of the high‑pt signal, and complement the handcrafted variables with modern sub‑structure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions, grooming‑based masses).  By iterating these refinements on an independent validation set and monitoring the background rate, we can gauge how much the vetoes can be safely loosened and whether a more flexible, data‑driven classifier can finally push the efficiency past the 0.65 threshold.
