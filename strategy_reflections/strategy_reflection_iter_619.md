# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 619 Report

Strategy vB1 succeeded largely because it replaced the overly restrictive static Gaussian mass‑resolution model with a pₜ‑dependent width that naturally tracks the growing jet‑energy smearing of boosted tops, thereby rescuing candidates that were previously penalised out of the selection; the use of the geometric mean of the two most W‑compatible dijet masses further protected genuine tops from being down‑weighted by a single mis‑measured jet, while the smooth logistic turn‑on applied to both the raw BDT score and the triplet pₜ avoided harsh threshold effects that can create efficiency cliffs. The added asymmetry factor efficiently suppressed combinatorial background by down‑weighting configurations with large spread among the three dijet masses, and the hard triplet‑mass window removed hopeless cases without sacrificing too many asymmetric signal tops. By combining these physics‑driven pieces in a non‑linear product, each term could dominate where it was most reliable, which is reflected in the achieved efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015—a clear improvement over the previous scheme. Still, the modest rise in mistag rate at intermediate pₜ and the residual loss of asymmetric tops suggest further refinements: calibrating the pₜ‑dependent Gaussian parameters directly on data, tuning the logistic thresholds, possibly replacing the fixed triplet‑mass window with a pₜ‑scaled acceptance, exploring alternative aggregation functions (e.g. a shallow neural network) to capture residual correlations, and incorporating complementary discriminants such as b‑tag scores or N‑subjettiness. Systematic validation on independent samples will be essential before the method is deployed in the full top‑quark analysis.
