# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 681 Report

Strategy v685 showed that the more sophisticated weighting and penalty scheme does sharpen the discrimination of genuine top‑triplets – the added log‑p T double‑Gaussian acceptance captured a modest boost in the ultra‑high‑p T tail and the mass‑pull/tanh term kept the bulk of well‑reconstructed tops from being overly punished – but the multiplicative combination of several harsh vetoes proved too aggressive, driving the overall true‑top efficiency down to 0.616 ± 0.015, well below the 0.65 goal (and even under the ≈0.64 barrier of the previous design). In particular, the asymmetry logistic penalty collapsed candidates with any one dijet mass far from the others, which is sensible for background suppression but also inadvertently discards signal events that naturally acquire larger mass fluctuations from final‑state radiation or imperfect jet clustering; likewise, the χ²‑to‑prob transformation, while elegant, sharply down‑weights moderately mismatched W‑candidates and the cube‑root compression can over‑compress useful variance. The next iteration should therefore relax the most punitive components – e.g. replace the strict multiplicative product with a weighted sum or introduce a tunable exponent on the asymmetry term – and re‑calibrate the pT‑dependent mass resolution of the pull term using an enlarged control sample to avoid over‑penalising high‑pT tops. A modest re‑optimization of the log‑Gaussian parameters (particularly the low‑pT tail) and a study of alternative “soft‑bonus” criteria (such as an N‑subjettiness or energy‑correlation cut) could recover lost signal without resurrecting the background surge, guiding the design toward the desired 0.65 efficiency while keeping the fake‑rate in check.
