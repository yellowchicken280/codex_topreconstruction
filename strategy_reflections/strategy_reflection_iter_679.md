# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 679 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % ± 1.5 % shows that the physics‑driven refinements in strategy X92 certainly moved the needle in the right direction—dynamic √pₜ‑scaled resolutions and the tanh‑softened χ² term rescued many high‑pₜ tops that the hard cut in B67 would have vetoed, while the asymmetry penalty and the 0.70‑power BDT exponent curbed a swathe of combinatorial fakes; however, the overall efficiency still fell short of the 65 % target because the Gaussian pₜ weight centered at 450 GeV is too narrow, over‑penalising genuine tops in the far tails, and the exponential asymmetry penalty appears to be overly aggressive for asymmetrically radiated, yet still correctly reconstructed, candidates; additionally, the pₜ‑dependent χ² scaling (1 + (pₜ/800)^0.6) may not fully capture the degradation of resolution at the extreme high‑pₜ end, leaving a residual bias that suppresses the very events the strategy was meant to recover.  The next iteration should therefore (i) broaden and possibly shift the Gaussian pₜ weighting (or replace it with a slowly varying polynomial) to give a smoother acceptance across the full pₜ spectrum, (ii) re‑tune the asymmetry penalty strength (e.g. introduce a tunable exponent or a piecewise‑linear form) to retain asymmetric but physical configurations, (iii) explore alternative soft‑clipping functions for the χ² term (such as a log‑sum‑exp) that preserve outlier down‑weighting without excessive flattening, and (iv) re‑optimise the BDT exponent and cut‑off values via a systematic hyper‑parameter scan on an enlarged validation sample, possibly augmenting the feature set with jet‑substructure observables; this focused recalibration is expected to push the reconstruction efficiency past the 65 % barrier while maintaining the robust background rejection already demonstrated.
