# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 705 Report

The modest 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency of strategy_typeB_v706 shows that the physics‑driven combination succeeded in retaining much of the boosted‑top signal but still fell short of the 64‑65 % target, likely because the added non‑linear penalties and the smooth tanh pT weighting, while protecting against hard cuts, ended up over‑constraining the candidate pool—especially the Gaussian product of the three dijet masses, which appears too narrow to accommodate realistic detector smearing and occasional jet‑pair mis‑assignments, and the final sigmoid, designed to avoid saturation, may be compressing useful discriminant variation back into a limited dynamic range; moreover, the logit conversion may have introduced a subtle bias in the linear combination with the χ² terms. To push the efficiency upward we should re‑optimize the width of the Gaussian mass terms (perhaps using adaptive kernels or a data‑driven calibration), loosen the tanh pT scaling to give more room for high‑pT events, explore a piecewise‑linear or calibrated soft‑max mapping instead of the shallow sigmoid, and incorporate additional substructure variables (e.g. N‑subjettiness or energy‑correlation functions) to improve the best‑candidate selection; a systematic hyper‑parameter scan on an independent validation set combined with a modest increase in BDT depth should help quantify how much each amendment contributes before finalising a new version of the Type‑B strategy.
