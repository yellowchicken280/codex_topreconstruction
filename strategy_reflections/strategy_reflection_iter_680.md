# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 680 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the Type‑B redesign moved the reconstruction well off the low‑efficiency plateau of X92—thanks largely to the double‑Gaussian pT weight (which rescued both modest‑ and ultra‑boosted tops) and the pT‑scaled W‑mass χ² term (which softened the harsh penalty that previously crippled the high‑pT tail), while the sigmoid asymmetry and tanh‑softened triplet‑mass χ² kept the algorithm from discarding physically plausible asymmetric radiation patterns. However, the gain fell short of the 0.65 goal, indicating that the broadened pT acceptance also let in more background‑like triplets, diluting the net gain, and that the chosen sigmoidal turn‑on (≈20 GeV spread) and the BDT exponent (0.78) may still be overly aggressive for the most extreme topologies. The next step should be a systematic scan of the double‑Gaussian mixture parameters (weights and σ₂) to fine‑tune the balance between acceptance and purity, together with a recalibration of the pT‑resolution exponent (currently 0.6) using data‑driven jet‑resolution studies. A modest tightening of the sigmoid asymmetry threshold (or switching to a piecewise linear penalty) and revisiting the BDT power‑compression (perhaps via a learned monotonic mapping) could further sharpen the ranking. Finally, implementing a pT‑binned veto (tight W‑mass window at low pT, looser at high pT) and validating the effect on efficiency vs. pT will reveal whether the high‑boost region finally reaches the desired 65 % level.
