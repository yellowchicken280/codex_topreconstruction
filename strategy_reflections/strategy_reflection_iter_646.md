# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 646 Report

While the Gaussian‑based mass likelihoods and the sigmoid low‑pT boost succeeded in making the physics score smoother and giving low‑pT jets a chance to contribute, the net efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 is still below the ≈0.64 baseline and far from the 0.65 goal, indicating that the new components introduced too much conservatism: the strict discarding of the third dijet partner removes a non‑negligible fraction of correctly reconstructed tops, the Gaussian widths chosen for the W‑ and top‑mass terms are probably too narrow so that modest detector smearing pushes many candidates out of the high‑probability region, and the fixed 55 % low‑pT physics weight together with the geometric‑mean coupling still leaves the BDT dominant where it is poorly calibrated, effectively re‑imposing the original mis‑modelling bias; the rescue factor based on a 5 GeV W‑mass window also appears insufficient to compensate for these losses. To move forward, a systematic hyper‑parameter scan of the Gaussian σ’s, the sigmoid turn‑on point and slope, and the physics‑vs‑BDT mixing fraction should be undertaken, possibly replacing the static geometric mean with a learnable combination (e.g., a shallow neural network) that can adapt to pT‑dependent BDT performance; re‑introducing a soft weight for the third dijet pair rather than outright removal, and validating the likelihood models on a dedicated low‑pT control sample, will also help to recover the missing efficiency and bring the reconstruction rate toward the 65 % target.
