# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 654 Report

The jump to a 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the physics‑driven tweaks in strategy_v654 paid off: by scoring each dijet pair with a narrow (σ≈10 GeV) Gaussian around m_W and selecting the best candidate before feeding it to the BDT, we rescued the washed‑out W‑mass peak that crippled the previous version, while the second Gaussian on the three‑jet mass (σ≈15 GeV) restored a crisp top‑mass resonance; the switch to multiplicative blending let genuine signal features amplify the raw BDT output instead of being swallowed by additive penalties, and the tanh‑scaled p_T factor together with the modest ratio boost (1 + 0.2·p_T/(m_{ij}^{best}+30)) rewarded the highly‑boosted topologies characteristic of real tops without letting the score runaway. The baseline term (0.3 + 0.7·weight) also prevented catastrophic loss of marginal events, and the two‑stage veto efficiently culled the most egregious background while still keeping borderline signal. Nonetheless, the remaining ∼38 % loss indicates that the current veto window (150–200 GeV) and the 30 GeV W‑compatibility tolerance may still be either too aggressive for some legitimate signal or too permissive for background, and the fixed Gaussian widths ignore the event‑by‑event resolution variations that could sharpen the peaks further. Going forward, we should calibrate σ values and the W‑mass offset on data‑driven jet‑energy resolution, explore a dynamic σ that scales with jet‑p_T or b‑tag information, fine‑tune the baseline weight (perhaps lowering the 0.3 floor) to avoid rescuing background, and replace the simple ratio boost with a more nuanced boost that also incorporates angular separation and b‑tag scores; adding a third‑level veto using event‑level variables such as missing E_T, lepton isolation, or the χ² of a full kinematic fit could tighten background rejection, and finally a cross‑validation study (e.g. k‑fold ROC analysis) will verify that the gains generalise and are not a statistical fluctuation before we lock the new configuration into production.
