# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 638 Report

The modest 61.6 %±1.5 % efficiency of strategy_v638 shows that while the new ingredients – the soft‑max W‑likelihood, logistic low‑pT rescue, Gaussian spread/ratio penalties, b‑jet consistency term and the two‑stage asymmetry veto – succeeded in taming the dominant combinatorial and pT‑cut inefficiencies that plagued v637, their combined multiplicative implementation and the sub‑unit exponent on the raw BDT over‑constrained many otherwise acceptable top triplets, especially those with a slightly broader dijet‑mass spread or asymmetric jet pT configuration; the Gaussian widths were likely too narrow given realistic jet‑energy resolution, and the exponential veto penalised legitimate topologies, resulting in an overall acceptance still below the 64 % target; to move forward we should perform a systematic hyper‑parameter scan to loosen the Gaussian penalties and asymmetry exponent, increase the temperature of the soft‑max and the amplitude of the logistic rescue, experiment with an additive rather than purely multiplicative blending of the physics terms with the BDT, and conduct an ablation study to quantify each term’s contribution, possibly augment the feature set with per‑event mass‑resolution estimates or stronger b‑tagging discriminants, and validate these refinements on independent data sets to finally push the reconstruction efficiency beyond the 64 % barrier.
