# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 663 Report

The modest yet statistically significant gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 in top‑tag efficiency shows that smoothing the hard 30 GeV W‑mass cut with a pT‑dependent Gaussian and a tanh(pT) up‑weight does indeed rescue a slice of genuinely high‑boost tops whose reconstructed W masses are broadened by jet‑substructure effects, while preserving a sensible probability interpretation; the multiplicative balance term further prunes the ubiquitous two‑pair‑near‑W background and the outlier veto keeps pathological candidates from dominating the ranking. At the same time the result hints at residual losses: the w_balance factor may be over‑penalising events where both dijet combinations sit near the W pole (as can happen in overlapping top decays), and raising the raw BDT score to a power >1 amplifies any statistical noise or systematic mismodelling in the original BDT, possibly inflating background leakage. Going forward the most promising refinements are to calibrate the Gaussian width and tanh scaling directly on data‑driven jet‑mass resolution versus pT, to scan the exponent of w_balance and the BDT‑power β for an optimal trade‑off between signal recovery and background suppression, and to augment the feature set with sub‑structure observables (e.g. τ21, energy‑correlation functions) that can be fed into a second‑stage BDT or a likelihood‑based combiner; finally, a systematic validation on independent MC generators and on early‐run data would verify the robustness of the new weighting scheme before any further hyper‑parameter tinkering.
