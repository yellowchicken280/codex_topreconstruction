# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 624 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the adaptive approach has indeed reclaimed roughly the ∼2 % of signal lost to the rigid legacy weighting, confirming that the three physics‑driven tweaks work as intended: a pT‑dependent σ widens the top‑mass Gaussian just enough to accommodate the degraded resolution of highly‑boosted jets, the exponential decay of the asymmetry exponent stops the veto from over‑penalising the inevitable increase in dijet asymmetry at high boost, and the new energy‑balance variable (Σ mᵢⱼ / m₁₂₃) supplies an orthogonal handle that further separates genuine three‑prong top decays from random triplets, while the two‑stage veto and soft tanh pT factor smooth the acceptance curve and purge pathological candidates early. The modest statistical uncertainty suggests the gain is real, but the added complexity also raises questions about possible over‑tuning, residual correlations between the new balance variable and the existing mass windows, and how the fake‑rate behaves across the full pT spectrum; therefore the next step should be a systematic study of background rejection (including data‑driven fake‑rate validation), a hyper‑parameter scan of the σ(pT) and asymmetry‑exponent decay constants, exploration of alternative functional forms (e.g. logistic or spline scaling) to reduce potential over‑fits, and finally integration of complementary sub‑structure observables (N‑subjettiness, soft‑drop mass) into a multivariate classifier so that the adaptive pieces can be quantitatively weighted against a broader feature set while keeping the overall algorithm tractable and robust against detector systematic variations.
