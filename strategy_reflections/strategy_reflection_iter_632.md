# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 632 Report

The new strategy_B_65 did confirm that the overly aggressive three‑mass Gaussian, hard χ² spread penalty, and tanh‑power BDT were indeed pruning genuine tops—by replacing them with smoother, physics‑motivated weights the true‑top efficiency rose modestly from the low‑60 % region to 0.616 ± 0.015, showing a clear, albeit insufficient, recovery of the ≈1 % signal loss that had been anticipated; however, the shortfall relative to the 0.65 goal can be traced to a few lingering bottlenecks: the weighted geometric mean still down‑weights events with marginal p_T or slightly off‑peak dijet masses, the RMS spread penalty remains too punitive for legitimate combinatorial fluctuations, the logistic p_T weight (centered at ≈200 GeV with a steep turn‑on) harshly penalises a sizable tail of true tops below this threshold, and the “single best‑W‑pair” selection can discard the correct pairing when extra radiation spoils the mass ordering, while the simple logistic vetoes occasionally reject low‑mass but genuine tops. To push the efficiency toward the 65 % target we should (i) re‑tune the Gaussian σ and logistic p_T parameters (e.g. broaden the σ to ≈2.5 GeV and flatten the logistic slope) using a grid or Bayesian optimisation under a fixed background‑rejection constraint, (ii) replace the hard vetoes with soft probability cuts that retain borderline candidates, (iii) adopt a more flexible BDT calibration (such as isotonic regression or Platt scaling) to preserve monotonicity without oversmoothing, (iv) consider an additive or weighted‑sum combination instead of a pure geometric mean to give a more balanced contribution from each term, and (v) enrich the score with complementary sub‑structure observables (τ₃/τ₂, energy‑correlation functions) in a multivariate fusion that can rescue events where the dijet‑mass‑based W‑tag is ambiguous. A systematic cross‑validation on independent simulated samples, together with a study of the background‑rejection trade‑off, will determine whether these refinements yield a statistically significant move beyond the current 0.616 efficiency while maintaining the desired background suppression.
