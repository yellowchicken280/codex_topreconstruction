# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 671 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015, while confirming that the physics‑driven weighting scheme can sharpen the signal‑background frontier, also reveals that the added non‑linearities over‑constrain the candidate pool and sacrifice genuine tops that fall outside the idealized windows; the hybrid Gaussian–Student‑t kernel successfully rescued a few outlier mass‑compatible jets but, because it entered multiplicatively, any modest deviation in the heavy‑tail component drags the whole score down, and the pₜ‑dependent sigmoid, tuned to favor the 300–500 GeV “sweet spot”, ends up down‑weighting a non‑negligible tail of correctly reconstructed tops at higher pₜ where the BDT still carries discriminating power. Moreover, the asymmetry weight, though conceptually sound, is too aggressive on the natural kinematic spread of W→jj decays, and the hard 30 GeV W‑mass veto removes a fraction of signal events with legitimate resolution smearing; finally, the tanh remapping, while expanding the high‑score tail, compresses the moderate‑score region where many borderline signal events reside, causing a loss of efficiency that outweighs the gain from reduced background. Going forward we will (i) replace the pure product of weights with a learned additive or calibrated combination to avoid “one‑bad‑factor” collapse, (ii) re‑optimize the sigmoid turnover points and introduce a softer pₜ penalty rather than a binary boost, (iii) soften the asymmetry criterion (e.g. using a Gaussian penalty on the mass ratio) and relax the W‑mass veto to ≈ 25 GeV with a smooth penalty function, (iv) fine‑tune the Student‑t heavy‑tail hyper‑parameters on dedicated resolution‑studies, and (v) supplement the score with auxiliary substructure and b‑tag probability variables and let a secondary gradient‑boosted regressor learn the optimal weighting, all validated with a full cross‑validation to ensure that the final efficiency pushes past the 64 % barrier toward the 65 % target.
