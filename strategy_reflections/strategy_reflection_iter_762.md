# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 762 Report

The new physics‑driven likelihood approach indeed softened the harsh cut‑offs of the previous pipeline, as evidenced by the modest gain in efficiency relative to the baseline (∼1.5–2 % absolute): the dynamic χ² term and Gaussian top_prob restored acceptance for low‑pT candidates, while the sub‑linear BDT shaping prevented extreme scores from dominating the decision. Nonetheless, the overall efficiency (0.616 ± 0.015) remains below the 65 % target, suggesting that the additional penalties—particularly the spread_weight that heavily down‑weights triplets with inconsistent dijet masses—are still too aggressive for a sizeable fraction of genuine tops whose jet‑energy resolution or combinatorial ambiguities smear the W‑mass peak. Moreover, the rational pt_weight denominator (120 GeV) may be too low to fully rescue moderate‑pT tops, and the fixed exponent 0.72 may not be optimal across the full pT spectrum. Future work should therefore explore a more flexible parametrisation: tune the strength of spread_weight (or replace it with a χ²‑probability term), scan the pt_weight scale, and consider learning the BDT exponent from data; alternatively, a simple calibration layer (e.g. isotonic regression) applied to the combined score could preserve background rejection while boosting signal efficiency. Systematic validation on independent samples and a detailed study of the χ² resolution model will guide these refinements.
