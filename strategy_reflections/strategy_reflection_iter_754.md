# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 754 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency gain of “strategy_v754” stems chiefly from replacing the rigid multiplicative gate with continuous, pₜ‑dependent probability factors that let a candidate survive even when a single pull (e.g. the W‑mass residual) would have killed it, and from the two‑pronged scoring architecture that lets mass‑consistency dominate at low pₜ while the raw BDT and a pₜ acceptance term take over at high boost; the logistic mixing weight therefore allocates the right information to the right kinematic regime, while the added dijet‑mass‑ratio and variance‑based consistency terms recover ultra‑boosted tops whose W‑pair residuals are inflated by detector smearing. The smooth gating also mitigates over‑penalisation and reduces binary decision loss, explaining the observed uplift. However, the gain comes at the cost of added complexity and potential over‑tuning of the logistic shape, the pull widths, and the variance scaling, which may affect background rejection and systematic stability. The next step should be a systematic validation on an independent simulated sample and on data control regions, accompanied by a thorough hyper‑parameter scan (logistic slope, pₜ‑broadened pull widths, Cauchy scale) to quantify the purity‑efficiency trade‑off, followed by studies of pile‑up and detector‑resolution systematics; if needed, a modest regularisation (e.g. Bayesian priors on the mixing weight) or a learned mixing function (via a shallow neural net) could be explored to further sharpen performance without sacrificing robustness.
