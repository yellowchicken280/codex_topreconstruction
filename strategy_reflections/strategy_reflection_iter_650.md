# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 650 Report

The modest gain of strategy_v650—reaching 61.6 % ± 1.5 % efficiency but still falling short of the 64 % target—shows that the multi‑stage, non‑linear design succeeded in taming the most egregious over‑penalisation of the earlier product (by smoothing the mass likelihood, softening the pₜ weighting and bounding the BDT output) and in pulling down the combinatorial background through the early 5 σ W‑mass veto, yet the trade‑off between background rejection and signal retention was still too aggressive: the logistic transform dampens the discriminating power of the raw BDT, the 150 GeV saturation scale of the rational pₜ term still suppresses a sizable slice of genuine medium‑pₜ tops, and the fixed 35/45/20 % log‑space weights may not be optimal for the full kinematic spectrum. Going forward the next step should be a systematic re‑tuning of these hyper‑parameters—e.g. scanning the pₜ saturation constant, exploring a softer sigmoid or piecewise linear pₜ weight, and allowing the BDT contribution to vary with its calibrated probability rather than a fixed logistic mapping—while also testing a more flexible combination scheme such as a small neural‑network meta‑classifier that can learn the optimal non‑linear weighting from data. Parallel to this, enriching the likelihood model with per‑candidate mass resolutions (full χ² rather than a single Gaussian) and adding complementary observables (ΔR of jet pairs, b‑tag scores) could restore some of the lost signal efficiency. Finally, a cross‑validation loop on independent MC samples will quantify whether the revised weighting improves the median efficiency without inflating the background rate, guiding the next iteration toward the desired > 64 % reconstruction performance.
