# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 633 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency indicates that the softer, physics‑motivated weighting scheme indeed rescued many genuine tops that were previously discarded by the harsh RMS spread and steep pT cut, confirming that the Gaussian‑based W‑mass and top‑mass likelihoods and the broad logistic pT promotion successfully reduced binary penalties and made the selector tolerant of realistic radiation and detector smearing; however, the modest gain (only a few percent above the baseline) suggests that the early‑veto stage is still clipping a non‑negligible fraction of true tops—especially those with borderline pT or slightly distorted dijet masses—while the multiplicative log‑space combination may be over‑penalising events that score poorly on any single term; the next step should therefore be a systematic retuning of the veto thresholds (e.g. lower pT floor, relaxed “all‑far‑from‑W” condition) and a refinement of the logistic pT parameters (mid‑point and width) to better preserve low‑pT tops, possibly replacing the strict product with a learned non‑linear aggregation (e.g. a shallow neural net or calibrated BDT) that can compensate for occasional weak terms, and finally validating the revised scheme on an independent sample to ensure that the observed efficiency improvement is robust and not the result of over‑fitting the current dataset.
