# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 675 Report

The modest 61.6 % ± 1.5 % efficiency indicates that the physics‑driven soft‑penalty scheme of strategy_v675 succeeded in softening the hard vetoes but over‑compensated for the very events it was meant to rescue – especially the high‑pₜ tops whose resolution degrades and for which the pₜ‑dependent top‑mass width was likely too broad, diluting the Gaussian likelihood and together with the asymmetry penalty (which heavily down‑weights any spread among the three dijet masses) ended up suppressing genuine signal triples as often as the original vetos suppressed background; in addition, the multiplicative fusion of these penalties with the raw BDT score can squash the discriminant’s dynamic range, and the final tanh stretch, while bounding the output, further compresses the tail where the best separation resides, leading to a net loss of rank‑ordering power. The next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan: tighten the Gaussian σ for the dijet masses, calibrate the pₜ‑dependent width scaling (perhaps using a piece‑wise linear rather than a simple functional form), and test an additive rather than multiplicative combination (or a shallow meta‑classifier that learns optimal weights) before re‑applying the tanh stretch; simultaneously, re‑weight the asymmetry penalty so that modest variations—expected from radiation and detector effects—are tolerated, and validate the tuned model on independent kinematic slices to ensure that the high‑pₜ regime truly benefits without sacrificing overall background rejection.
