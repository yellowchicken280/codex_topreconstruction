# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 622 Report

The modest rise to 61.6 % (≈2 σ below the 64 % goal) shows that the pT‑aware soft‑minimum, sigmoidal asymmetry damping and pT‑scaled Gaussian window do indeed recover a non‑trivial share of tops that were discarded by the rigid W‑mass and asymmetry cuts, confirming that dynamic, kinematics‑dependent penalties soften the decision boundary without collapsing background rejection; however, the gain stalls because the current parametrisations are still too conservative for the most boosted regime (the sigmoid’s turn‑on point and slope limit the allowed asymmetry, and a linear width growth cannot fully absorb the rapidly worsening mass resolution), and the multiplicative combination still excessively down‑weights candidates that carry modest but correlated background‑like signals. The next iteration should therefore (i) re‑fit the sigmoid parameters (or replace it with an adaptive logistic that tracks the ISR‑driven asymmetry spread as a function of pT), (ii) explore a higher‑order or piecewise pT dependence for the Gaussian width (e.g. quadratic scaling above 500 GeV), (iii) adjust the weight of the W‑likeness boost (perhaps additive rather than multiplicative) to prevent over‑penalisation of events with a good W pair but a slightly off‑mass top, and (iv) augment the feature set with jet‑substructure observables (τ₃/τ₂, energy‑correlation ratios) and per‑event resolution estimates so that the BDT can learn residual correlations; a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (including cross‑validation on an independent validation set) will then reveal whether these refinements can push the efficiency past the 64 % threshold while preserving the background rejection achieved so far.
