# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 692 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the physics‑driven refinements in strategy_v693 were on the right track – the dynamic widening of the W‑ and top‑mass sigmas successfully rescued high‑pₜ candidates that the previous fixed windows would have discarded, and the smooth Gaussian for the triplet mass prevented an abrupt loss of slightly off‑peak tops; however, the overall efficiency still falls short of the 0.64 goal, suggesting that the combination of non‑linear exponents and the chosen penalty strengths over‑suppresses events with moderate mass spread, and that the hard veto on dijet spreads > 60 GeV may be too aggressive for many genuine tops. A more systematic hyper‑parameter optimisation (e.g. grid, Bayesian, or evolutionary search) of the exponent values and penalty coefficients, as well as a pₜ‑dependent or softened spread cut, could restore some of the lost signal. In parallel, augmenting the handcrafted score with additional shape observables (such as N‑subjettiness ratios or energy‑correlation functions) or feeding the existing terms into a shallow neural‑network to learn an optimal non‑linear combination may further boost discrimination. The next step should therefore be an ablation study to quantify each component’s impact, followed by a focused re‑tuning of the sigmoid pₜ weight and penalties, and validation on an independent dataset to ensure the improvements are robust and not artefacts of over‑fitting.
