# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 732 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % ± 1.5 % shows that the new ingredients – a log‑dependent σ_top that softens the high‑pₜ turn‑on, the “best‑W” Gaussian weight, the spread‑factor veto and the steep logistic squashing of the BDT – each helped recover some semi‑boosted signal, while the pₜ‑dependent exponent and the tanh rescue factor restored a bit of efficiency in the extreme boost region; however, the overall target of >64 % remains unmet because the spread‑veto Gaussian is still too punitive for legitimate jet‑energy fluctuations (especially when the three dijet masses differ modestly due to resolution), the logistic centre at 0.2 over‑compresses midscore candidates, and the exponent range (≈0.8–1.2) does not increase fast enough to fully counteract the residual Gaussian suppression at the highest pₜ. The next iteration should therefore re‑tune the spread penalty width (e.g. broaden the σ of the Gaussian or replace it with a softened piece‑wise function), explore a slightly lower logistic midpoint or a shallower slope to retain more midscore events, and extend the pₜ‑exponent scaling (perhaps up to ≈1.4 at pₜ > 600 GeV) while ensuring it does not re‑introduce fake‑rate inflation; additionally, a data‑driven calibration of σ_top versus pₜ and an expanded feature set (e.g. N‑subjettiness ratios or energy‑correlation functions) fed into a re‑trained BDT or a lightweight graph‑NN could provide the extra discriminating power needed to push the efficiency past the 64 % barrier without sacrificing background rejection.
