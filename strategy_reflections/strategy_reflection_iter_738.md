# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 738 Report

The modest gain delivered by strategy_v738 shows that the physics‑driven smoothing and p_T‑dependent weighting do indeed rescue some midsize tops that were previously thrown away by hard vetoes—evidenced by the uplift from the baseline χ²‐only discriminant—and the spread‑penalty successfully curtails the most egregious combinatorial triplets, sharpening the BDT’s separation power at high p_T; however, the overall integrated efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 remains ≈1.6 σ below the 0.64 target, indicating that the new components are still too aggressive in regions where genuine signal fluctuates (e.g. the σ≈10 GeV W‑mass term may be overly tight for lower‑p_T jets, and the p_T‑dependent exponent can amplify statistical noise in the high‑p_T tail). The plateau of the sigmoid gate may also be narrower than optimal, cutting into the 150–350 GeV window that houses the bulk of the signal. Next steps should therefore focus on a systematic hyper‑parameter scan: loosen the W‑mass σ, broaden the sigmoid transition widths, and re‑tune the spread‑penalty strength using a grid or Bayesian optimizer on a dedicated validation set; concurrently, calibrate the BDT output against a data‑driven control region to guard against over‑fitting, and explore adding complementary substructure observables (e.g. N‑subjettiness) or a shallow neural‑network layer to capture residual correlations that the current analytic terms miss. By iterating these refinements and re‑evaluating both statistical and systematic uncertainties, we can aim to push the integrated efficiency over the 0.65 threshold while preserving robust background rejection.
