# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 755 Report

The modest but statistically significant lift to 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the physics‑driven, multi‑stage gate succeeded in rescuing a slice of otherwise marginal candidates – especially in the high‑pT regime where the raw BDT under‑scores due to altered sub‑structure – by smoothing hard cuts into continuous penalties (the Gaussian pull suppressions, the exp(–½ χ²_W) gate and the tanh‑boost weight) and by penalising overly disparate dijet masses that usually betray combinatorial mishaps; however, the early stage veto on any W‑pull > 2.5 σ for low‑pT tops also removed a few genuine events, and the fixed widths of the Gaussian pulls and the static exponent on χ² may be too restrictive for the tail of the boosted spectrum, limiting the overall gain. The next step should therefore be a systematic scan of the gate hyper‑parameters (pull σ, χ² exponent, tanh scale, top‑gate tolerance) possibly guided by a small Bayesian optimizer, combined with a per‑pT bin performance audit to quantify where the gate helps versus where it hurts; parallelly, embedding these physics terms as explicit features in a second‑level learner (e.g. a shallow neural net or a calibrated gradient‑boosted tree) could let the algorithm discover a more optimal non‑linear combination while preserving the intuitive physical constraints, and a thorough background‑rejection study will ensure that the modest efficiency boost does not come at an unacceptable cost in purity.
