# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 735 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the physics‑driven pₜ‑adaptation ideas are on the right track—scaling the top‑mass Gaussian width with a tanh‑interpolated σ and rewarding high‑pₜ candidates with a smooth boost clearly rescued a fraction of otherwise discarded boosted tops, and the averaged W‑mass weights plus a gentle variance penalty helped keep the dijet mass consistency under control—yet the overall efficiency still falls short of the 64 % goal because the penalty terms and the softened BDT exponent appear to over‑suppress signal in the moderate‑pₜ regime and the σ‑scaling may not be aggressive enough at the highest pₜ.  To push past the barrier the next step should be a focused hyper‑parameter sweep: increase the reduction of σ at large pₜ (or try a piecewise linear instead of tanh scaling), lower the weight of the variance penalty, and experiment with a pₜ‑dependent BDT exponent that grows (rather than shrinks) for the most boosted jets; alternatively, train separate BDTs for low‑ and high‑pₜ bins and combine them with a pₜ‑dependent mixing.  Adding substructure observables (e.g. τ₃/τ₂, subjet‑b‑tag scores) into the discriminant could further distinguish genuine tops from background, and a detailed per‑pₜ efficiency study would reveal where the current formulation still penalises signal.  By iterating these refinements and validating on an independent sample, we can aim to lift the signal efficiency above the 64 % threshold while preserving the intended background rejection.
