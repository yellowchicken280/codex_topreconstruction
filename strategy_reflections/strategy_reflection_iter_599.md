# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 599 Report

Although strategy_v599 nudged the top‑tagging efficiency up to 0.616 ± 0.015 – a clear improvement over the plain‑BDT baseline – it still missed the 0.65 target, chiefly because the Gaussian copula only partially restored the lost joint probability: the assumed correlation (ρ≈0.45) underestimated the true dependence between the three‑jet mass and the dijet‑to‑W deviation, leaving a residual mismatch that the weighted geometric mean could not fully compensate, and the tanh scaling for low‑pT tops proved too gentle while the sigmoid‑type vetoes were still harsh enough to discard marginally consistent candidates, especially when the BDT weight remained dominant; consequently many genuinely reconstructable tops that the BDT down‑ranked remained suppressed. To bridge the gap we should (i) re‑estimate ρ on high‑statistics simulation or adopt a more flexible t‑copula/vine‑copula to capture heavier tails, (ii) re‑optimise the exponents (w_bdt, w_joint, w_pt) via a Bayesian hyper‑parameter search that explicitly maximises efficiency under a regularised loss, (iii) replace the hand‑crafted tanh/sigmoid maps with data‑driven monotonic splines or learned activation functions that can be calibrated on a validation set, and (iv) enrich the joint likelihood with additional observables (ΔR, subjet multiplicity, angular correlations) or embed the whole combination in a shallow neural network that learns the full non‑linear interplay, followed by rigorous cross‑validation on an independent hold‑out sample to confirm a robust gain toward the 65 % goal.
