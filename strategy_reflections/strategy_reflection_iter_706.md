# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 706 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % shows that the physics‑driven relaxations did indeed rescue a slice of genuinely boosted tops that were previously thrown away by the overly tight Gaussian windows, especially the adaptive σW and the Student‑t mass penalties, but the multi‑stage veto (2.5 σ) and the relatively aggressive dijet‑mass‑ratio regulariser still trim away a non‑negligible tail of good candidates; furthermore, the fixed exponent weights (0.7, 0.4, 0.5, 0.6, 0.3) give the raw BDT too much dominance, limiting the benefit of the new kinematic terms, and the arctan pT scaling may under‑estimate the resolution growth at the highest pT. To push the efficiency past the 64 % goal we should (i) relax the hard veto to ≈2.8 σ or replace it with a soft penalty, (ii) re‑optimise the Student‑t degrees of freedom and the σ(pT) parametrisations by a small grid or Bayesian search, (iii) tune the combination exponents (or learn them via a shallow neural‑net) so the mass‑based scores get a larger share, (iv) recalibrate the final sigmoid (threshold ≈ 0.52, steepness ≈ 10) to better match the operating point, and (v) explore adding complementary discriminants such as N‑subjettiness or jet‑pull variables that could further distinguish correct pairings from pathological hierarchies. A systematic hyper‑parameter scan on a validation set, followed by a cross‑checked evaluation on the full simulation, will tell whether these refinements close the remaining efficiency gap.
