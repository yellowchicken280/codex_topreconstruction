# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 752 Report

The pt‑dynamic version succeeded in softening the hard 30 GeV W‑mass veto and in modelling the deteriorating dijet‑mass resolution at very high boost, which allowed a few genuine ultra‑boosted tops that were previously discarded to be recovered; however, by letting the Gaussian widths grow logarithmically and by averaging the three dijet Gaussian weights instead of focusing on the best W candidate, the discriminating power in the bulk (moderate‑pT) region was simultaneously weakened, and the relaxed outlier penalty for p_T>400 GeV let more combinatorial background survive, so the net gain was insufficient to push the overall efficiency past the 0.64 target, leaving it at 0.616 ± 0.015. The next step should be a more nuanced optimisation: keep the p_T‑dependent σ scaling but revert to a max‑weight (or weighted‑average with a bias toward the best pairing) for the W‑mass term, fine‑tune the sigmoid p_T gate (steeper rise or lower midpoint) and make the outlier factor a smoother function of p_T that only relaxes above ≈500 GeV, and augment the score with complementary sub‑structure observables (e.g. τ_32, soft‑drop mass) and an explicit b‑tag weight. A systematic scan of these parameters on a validation sample—potentially guided by a lightweight BDT to capture non‑linear correlations—will reveal whether the ultra‑boosted recovery can be amplified without eroding the mid‑range purity and thereby finally break the 64 % efficiency barrier.
