# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 749 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the three‑pronged redesign of strategy_v749 is indeed paying off: the early pT‑adaptive veto efficiently excises the long tail of off‑mass QCD triplets, preserving most genuine W candidates and instantly sharpening the background shape; the non‑linear exponent mixing wisely lets the high‑pT regime lean on the already‑well‑trained BDT (where detector resolution is optimal) while falling back on the physics‑driven likelihood at low pT, thereby avoiding the BDT’s over‑sensitivity to noisy low‑energy features; finally, the smooth pT gate together with the sigmoid‑steepened BDT pushes well‑reconstructed tops into the discriminator’s most discriminating region, which translates directly into the observed efficiency gain.  The remaining ∼4 % statistical uncertainty hints that the gains are not yet saturated—further tightening of the 3σ mass veto or smoothing of the gate could still prune residual background, but one must guard against over‑cutting signal, especially in the intermediate‑pT window where the exponent mix is most delicate.  The next step is a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (σ‑cut, gate slope, exponent shape) possibly guided by Bayesian optimisation, combined with an out‑of‑sample validation on a fully independent Monte‑Carlo set and, if available, early data‑driven control regions, to quantify any over‑training and to fine‑tune the trade‑off between purity and efficiency before deploying the upgraded tagger in physics analyses.
