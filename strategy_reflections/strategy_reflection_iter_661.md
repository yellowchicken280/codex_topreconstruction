# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 661 Report

The modest but statistically significant rise to ε=0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the physics‑driven refinements in strategy_v661 paid off: by replacing the overly aggressive linear σ∝pT with a slower √pT growth the mass‑likelihood term better matched the true resolution, the summed‑log‑likelihood treatment of the three dijet W candidates preserved signal when one pair drifted, and the pT‑dependent weight shift smoothly transferred emphasis from a deteriorating mass term to the more robust BDT discriminator at high boost, while the softened BDT scaling and soft veto kept borderline events that the previous hard 3σ cut would have thrown away. The remaining inefficiency likely stems from residual background leakage in the high‑pT regime where the BDT’s discriminating power is still limited, as well as from the empirically chosen functional forms (√pT scaling, power‑law BDT softening, 0.2 down‑weight factor) that may not be optimal across the full kinematic range. The next step should be a systematic scan of these hyper‑parameters—e.g. fitting σ(pT) directly from calibration samples, tuning the BDT exponent and veto factor, and exploring alternative weighting functions (polynomial or spline‑based) that can be learned from data—while cross‑validating on independent simulated and, where possible, data control regions; simultaneously, incorporating additional substructure observables or a second‑stage multivariate combiner (e.g. a shallow neural network) could further sharpen the separation and translate the improved inclusive efficiency into a higher signal‑to‑background ratio.
