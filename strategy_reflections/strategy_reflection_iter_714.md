# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 714 Report

The modest gain achieved by strategy_v714—pushing the tagging efficiency from earlier values to 0.616 ± 0.0152—shows that the pT‑dependent Gaussian mass‑resolution kernels and the high‑pT envelope do indeed soften the overly‑rigid penalties of the original tanh/Gaussian formulation, rescuing a slice of moderate‑pT signal that was previously down‑weighted; however, the hard veto embedded in the geometric‑mean combination and the very steep sigmoid (centred at 0.25) still truncate a sizable tail of otherwise viable candidates, especially at the extremes of the pT spectrum where the resolution growth is underestimated. In practice the σ_top(pT) and σ_W(pT) scalings were calibrated on MC alone, so the rise in mass‐resolution at the highest boosts is still too conservative, causing genuine tops to be over‑penalised, while the fixed envelope centre (~250 GeV) does not fully recover low‑pT signal. To break the 0.64 barrier we should replace the binary veto with a smooth penalty (e.g. a log‑likelihood or soft‑max weighting), re‑tune the sigmoid’s midpoint and slope (perhaps using a temperature‑controlled logistic function), and refine the σ(pT) parametrisation with data‑driven resolution measurements (e.g. from Z → bb control samples). Adding complementary sub‑structure variables (groomed jet mass, N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation ratios) into the BDT and retraining it under the new weighting scheme will likely increase discrimination power. Finally, a systematic cross‑validation on an independent hold‑out set will quantify any over‑fitting and guide iterative optimisation of the envelope width and centre, ensuring that the final tagger consistently exceeds the 0.64 efficiency target while preserving the desired background rejection.
