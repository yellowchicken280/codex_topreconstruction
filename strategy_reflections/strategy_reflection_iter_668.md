# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 668 Report

The soft‑clip, additive‑log‑likelihood formulation did indeed soften the brutal step‑function vetoes that had been starving the sample – the χ² and dijet‑mass terms now give a graded penalty rather than an all‑or‑nothing kill, and the modest high‑pT boost term adds a gentle incentive for boosted tops – which is why we see the efficiency climb to 0.616 ± 0.0152, a clear improvement over the hard‑cut baseline. However, the gain falls short of the 0.65 goal because the penalty curves are still too steep in the regions where the majority of lost signal lives: the log1p‑exp transition for the χ² term is centred too close to the original cut, and the dijet‑mass soft‑clip still caps the weight too aggressively for masses just above 130 GeV, indicating residual over‑penalisation. Moreover, the additive log‑likelihood assumes independence of the ingredients, so correlations (e.g. between χ² and dijet‑mass) still lead to an effective double‑counting of bad‑fit events. The next iteration should therefore (1) re‑tune the soft‑clip scale parameters (e.g. increase the temperature/width of the log1p‑exp smoothing) to further flatten the penalty slope, (2) introduce a modest correlation term or a calibrated joint likelihood to mitigate double‑counting, (3) test alternative high‑pT reward functions (e.g. a piecewise‑log or a power‑law boost) that more strongly rewards the most energetic tops without inflating background, and (4) validate the revised weights on a dedicated control region to ensure that background rejection is not sacrificed while we push the reconstruction efficiency toward and beyond the 65 % target.
