# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 618 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % ± 1.5 % shows that the smooth, physics‑driven weighting idea was on the right track—dynamic Gaussian smearing correctly softened the harsh p_T‑dependent resolution penalty, and forming the product of the two most W‑compatible Gaussian weights rescued many asymmetric W→jj candidates that would have been lost under a single‑choice scheme, while the logistic turn‑on and asymmetry factor prevented the most egregious outliers from dominating the selection; however, the product of three independent weights still over‑suppresses events in which only one component is strong, the Gaussian width scaling appears too narrow for the highest‑p_T tops (so residual over‑penalisation remains), the asymmetry factor may be too aggressive for legitimate but naturally imbalanced top decays, and the logistic reshaping of the BDT score was not optimally tuned to the underlying score distribution, leaving room for further efficiency. The next step should be a systematic re‑optimisation of the individual calibrations: fit a p_T‑dependent σ for the top‑mass Gaussian directly to detector‐resolution curves, replace the strict product with a weighted sum or a max‑operator that preserves a strong individual term, fine‑tune the logistic parameters (midpoint and slope) via a grid or Bayesian search, soften the asymmetry penalty by introducing a continuous, data‑driven asymmetry function, and, if needed, augment the discriminant with additional orthogonal variables (e.g. jet‑substructure or angular correlations) or a shallow BDT trained on the weighted inputs. Cross‑validation on independent simulated samples will quantify whether these refinements can push the efficiency beyond the 64 % goal while keeping background under control.
