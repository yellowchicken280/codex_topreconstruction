# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 604 Report

The modest gain (≈61 % efficiency, still shy of the 64 % plateau) tells us that the new ingredients shifted the decision surface in the right direction but also introduced new sources of over‑penalisation: the Gaussian‑Student‑t mixture successfully softened the response for extreme mass tails, and the sigmoid pₜ weight rescued many low‑energy tops, yet the hard 3σ dijet‑mass veto and the ¹·² exponent on the raw BDT appear to have been too aggressive, discarding a non‑negligible fraction of genuine signal (especially in the intermediate‑pₜ region where the veto overlaps with the tail of the Gaussian core); the low‑pₜ rescue term, while conceptually sound, was likely under‑scaled relative to the BDT boost, so its uplift was insufficient to compensate for the harsher cuts.  The next iteration should therefore re‑tune the mixture’s ν (perhaps moving toward ν≈1.8), replace the fixed 3σ veto with a smooth, mass‑asymmetry‑weighted penalty, and explore a milder exponent (≈1.1) or a calibrated probability mapping for the BDT output; an ablation study to quantify each term’s impact, coupled with a tighter cross‑validation loop and the addition of complementary discriminants (e.g. b‑tag scores or jet‑substructure observables) will help balance background rejection against the soft‑top recovery needed to finally breach the 64 % barrier.
