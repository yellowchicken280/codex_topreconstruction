# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 697 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency of strategy_v695 shows that the idea of embedding smooth, physics‑motivated weights (χ²‑score, logistic pT boost and quadratic asymmetry penalty) into the BDT did improve the shape of the decision surface, but the multiplicative‐and‑veto structure ultimately over‑pruned genuine top candidates: the steep Gaussian conversion of the χ² term and the quadratic asym_penalty together produce a score that falls off too quickly for modest mass‐mis‑measurements, while the three staged vetoes—especially the hard χ² cut and the simultaneous‑far‑from‑W veto—re‑introduce effectively hard thresholds that discard a non‑negligible fraction of signal events that would otherwise be rescued by the BDT’s multivariate power. In hindsight, the smoothness of the individual components was partly nullified by the aggressive combination, and the BDT could not fully exploit the residual correlations. Going forward we should (i) relax the χ²‑to‑Gaussian scaling (e.g. use a softer polynomial or a wider σ), (ii) temper the asymmetry penalty (perhaps linear or a smaller quadratic coefficient), (iii) replace the multiplicative scheme with an additive or learned combination (e.g. letting the BDT receive the raw χ², pT‑logistic, and asymmetry as separate features rather than a pre‑product), and (iv) soften or eliminate the hard vetoes, instead feeding veto‑region flags as binary inputs so the tree can learn when to ignore them. Parallelly, we will perform a systematic hyper‑parameter sweep (grid or Bayesian optimisation) of the logistic boost slope, χ² σ, and asymmetry weight, and explore adding complementary high‑level variables (e.g. jet‑substructure N‑subjettiness, b‑tag scores, event‑wide kinematics) to give the BDT more levers to recover signal efficiency. If these refinements still fall short, we will consider switching to a deeper ensemble (e.g. XGBoost with deeper trees or a shallow neural network) that can model the non‑linear interplay of these physics‑driven features more flexibly.
