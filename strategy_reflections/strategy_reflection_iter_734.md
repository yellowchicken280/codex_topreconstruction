# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 734 Report

The new “strategy_ptpiecewise_v2” did raise the overall signal efficiency to 0.616 ± 0.0152 – an improvement over the previous linear‑weighting approach – because the pT‑modulated BDT steepening and the super‑linear pT boost successfully rescued high‑pT candidates that were previously suppressed, while the Gaussian mass rewards provided a smooth incentive for correctly reconstructed top‑ and W‑mass windows; however, the gain fell short of the 0.65 target mainly due to the overly harsh multiplicative synthesis, which still forces a candidate to excel simultaneously in every dimension and thus discards many otherwise acceptable high‑pT tops once the spread‑penalty, even with its tanh‑scaled tolerance, rejects events with the larger reconstruction smearing typical at high pT, and because the Gaussian width for the mass rewards is too narrow, penalising genuine mass fluctuations and limiting the net boost from the pT‑dependent exponent; to close the remaining gap we should (i) re‑tune the exponent‑ramp and the tanh‑scale of γ (e.g. via a Bayesian optimisation or grid scan) to soften the high‑pT boost just enough to outweigh background, (ii) replace the strict product of the components with a partially additive combination or introduce weighting factors that allow strong performance in a subset of variables to compensate for modest scores elsewhere, (iii) broaden or replace the single‑Gaussian mass rewards with a double‑Gaussian or kernel‑density estimate to retain signal tails, (iv) explore a dedicated high‑pT BDT or a shallow neural‑network layer to capture residual nonlinear correlations, and (v) validate each modification across fine pT bins to ensure that any efficiency gain does not come at the expense of background acceptance.
