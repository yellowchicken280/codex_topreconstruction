# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 741 Report

The 0.6160 ± 0.0152 efficiency obtained with strategy_v742 can be traced to the fact that we explicitly removed the three mechanisms that were choking low‑pT top candidates in the original selection: the hard χ² walls, the fixed‑width Gaussian W‑mass window, and the overly aggressive asymmetry penalty. By scaling the W‑mass resolution with pT the σ assigned to soft triplets now matches the detector’s true resolution, and the replacement of the hard window by a smooth log‑sum‑exp likelihood keeps any dijet combination that looks plausible rather than discarding it outright; the Student‑t model for the top‑mass term captures the long, non‑Gaussian tails seen in simulation, while damping the asymmetry term at low pT prevents genuine asymmetric decays from being penalised when the measurement is noisy. The lightweight second‑stage rescue further salvages well‑behaved low‑pT candidates without inflating the background, and all of these refinements sit on top of the original BDT score, preserving the powerful multivariate information while adding pT‑aware kinematic discriminants. The net effect is a substantial gain in signal acceptance, especially in the previously under‑represented low‑pT regime. To consolidate this improvement we should (i) validate the new selection on independent MC samples and data control regions to ensure the background rejection and systematic behaviour remain acceptable under varied pile‑up conditions, (ii) perform a systematic scan of the hyper‑parameters (the pT‑scaling factor for the W‑mass resolution, the temperature of the log‑sum‑exp, and the Student‑t degrees of freedom) to optimise the trade‑off between efficiency and purity, (iii) explore a dynamic weighting that lets the BDT contribution fade for low‑pT candidates where the new likelihood terms dominate, and (iv) integrate the rescue stage into a full likelihood fit so its effect can be calibrated directly on data. These studies will tell us whether v742 should become the default or whether further refinements—perhaps a matrix‑element or deep‑learning based top‑tagger that naturally incorporates pT‑dependent resolution effects—are warranted.
