# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 736 Report

Overall, strategy_B65_v001 nudged us in the right direction by sharpening the high‑pₜ top‑mass peak with a pₜ‑dependent σ and by enforcing tighter simultaneous consistency among the three dijet masses, but the added rigidity of the individual Gaussian weights, the aggressive spread‑penalty, and the hard top‑mass veto collectively over‑pruned the moderate‑pₜ regime where resolution gains are modest, which explains the modest 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency still falling short of the 0.65 goal; the sigmoid gating and pₜ‑scaled BDT exponent helped recover some boosted tops, yet their functional forms were likely too gentle or incorrectly centered, leaving a residual suppression of signal. The next step should be a systematic re‑tuning: calibrate the σ(pₜ) scaling to avoid excessive narrowing at intermediate pₜ, relax the spread‑penalty (or replace it with a continuous penalty term) to retain partially‑reconstructed tops, soften or move the hard veto threshold, and explore a more aggressive or piecewise BDT exponent that can be cross‑validated on separate pₜ bins; simultaneously, we should validate the Gaussian‑weight product against data‑driven templates to ensure realistic dijet‑mass correlations and consider augmenting the model with a substructure‑based discriminator or a shallow neural‑network tagger to capture residual boosted‑top information that the current analytic terms miss.
