# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 743 Report

Strategy v901 clearly succeeded in pulling the efficiency up to the 0.62 region, showing that the layered physics‑driven weighting (calibrated W‑mass likelihood, a Gaussian‑core/Student‑t tail for the top mass, soft pT‑dependent resolution, logistic pT boost, asymmetry penalty and a hard W‑mass veto) together with the raw BDT score can preserve discriminating power while keeping background in check; however, it fell just shy of the 0.65 target, suggesting that a few aspects are still limiting signal acceptance. The heavy‑tailed Student‑t term, while protecting against moderate outliers, may still truncate genuine high‑deviation signal events, and the conservative low‑pT resolution, though intended to avoid over‑smearing, appears to suppress low‑pT tops more than necessary. Moreover, the multiplicative combination can overly penalise events with a strong BDT score if any physics weight is low, and the fixed 40 GeV W‑mass veto, although effective at rejecting impossibly heavy Ws, removes a non‑negligible tail of true signal fluctuations. The next steps should therefore focus on systematic hyper‑parameter tuning of the non‑linear exponents and weight scales, exploring alternative heavy‑tailed forms (e.g. generalized Gaussian or asymmetric Laplace) for the top‑mass term, and replacing the hard veto with a dynamic, resolution‑scaled tolerance. Additionally, integrating the physics‑driven variables directly into the BDT training (or using a hybrid additive‑multiplicative scheme) could let the classifier learn the optimal trade‑offs, while cross‑validation across pT bins will ensure the soft resolution is correctly balanced—refinements that are expected to push efficiency past the 0.65 goal without inflating the background rate.
