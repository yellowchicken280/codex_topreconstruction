# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 698 Report

The jump to a 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that softening the hard cuts into Gaussian‑type mass‐consistency factors, a logistic pT boost, and an exponential asymmetry penalty indeed rescued many signal‑like triplets that the previous over‑pruned version would have discarded, confirming that a smooth, continuous weighting preserves the multivariate power of the raw BDT while tolerating realistic detector smearing; however, the residual loss points to the lingering rigidity of the final hard veto and perhaps an overly aggressive asymmetry term that still penalises legitimate top candidates with natural radiation‑induced mass spread, especially at moderate pT where the logistic boost provides less compensation. To push the performance further, the next step should be a systematic scan of the Gaussian widths, the steepness and saturation point of the pT boost, and the decay constant of the asymmetry exponential, replacing the binary veto with a soft penalty that can be tuned on control samples; simultaneously, adding complementary discriminants (e.g. b‑tag scores, sub‑jet grooming variables) and retraining the BDT—or swapping it for a more flexible deep‑set or graph‑based network—will let the algorithm learn optimal correlations and may lift the efficiency towards the target while keeping background rejection under control.
