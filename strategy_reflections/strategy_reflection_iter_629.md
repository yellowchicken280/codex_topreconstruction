# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 629 Report

The modest 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency indicates that the soft‑Cauchy upgrades succeeded in softening the harsh binary cuts that previously throttled moderate‑boost tops—indeed the heavy‑tailed Cauchy pull and the data‑driven σ(pₜ) decay rescued a noticeable fraction of low‑mid‑pₜ signal and the smooth‑max kept the gradient flow alive for the three dijet W hypotheses—but the additional regularisation pieces over‑compensated, curbing the net gain: the variance penalty on the three dijet masses proved too aggressive, rejecting genuine signal configurations whose masses naturally fluctuate because of detector resolution, while the low‑pₜ exponential boost asymptoted to unity too early, leaving the moderate‑boost region under‑enhanced; moreover, the smooth‑max can flatten the relative separation of competing W candidates, diminishing the discriminating power of the original BDT score and allowing more background to slip through the heavy‑tailed Cauchy tail. The next iteration should therefore (i) re‑tune the strength of the variance penalty (perhaps switching to a softer, variance‑scaled regulariser or a learned consistency term), (ii) explore a pₜ‑dependent boost that saturates later or adopts a piecewise functional form, (iii) optimise the Cauchy scale parameter and consider a hybrid Gaussian‑Cauchy kernel to keep background suppression while preserving signal tails, and (iv) experiment with alternative soft‑max alternatives such as log‑sum‑exp with temperature annealing to retain sharper candidate ranking; a systematic hyper‑parameter scan coupled with per‑pₜ bin ROC studies will reveal the optimal balance needed to push the efficiency past the 0.65 goal.
