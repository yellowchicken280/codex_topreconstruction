# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 617 Report

The 61.6 %±1.5 % efficiency shows that the multi‑stage, physics‑driven design of strategy_v650 is indeed capable of rescuing a sizable fraction of intermediate‑pT tops that simpler rectangular cuts would miss, confirming that the dynamic σ_top∝√p_T, the p_T‑scaled BDT turn‑on and the symmetric‑pair weighting all contribute useful discriminating power; however, the shortfall relative to the 64 % goal suggests that the early hard vetoes and the steep logistic down‑weighting of soft candidates are probably too aggressive, causing a non‑negligible loss of genuine tops especially near the veto thresholds, while the mass‑asymmetry weight may be over‑penalising legitimate asymmetric W→jj decays in the presence of detector smearing. The next step should therefore focus on systematic hyper‑parameter optimisation – e.g. relaxing the dijet‑mass and top‑mass veto windows, tuning the σ_top(p_T) scaling and the logistic centre/slope of the BDT weight, and re‑balancing the mass‑asymmetry exponent – while validating each tweak on an independent subsample to avoid over‑fitting; additionally, augmenting the discriminant with complementary observables such as b‑tag scores, jet‑substructure variables or a modest deep‑learning layer (e.g. a graph‑network on constituent particles) could capture residual correlations and further close the efficiency gap without compromising background rejection.
