# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 601 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the heavy‑tailed Student‑t penalty and the p_T‑modulated exponent successfully rescued a subset of low‑p_T tops that were previously over‑penalised, but the overall gain fell short of the 0.64 target because the early‑veto cut and the aggressive sigmoid (k = 8, offset = 0.5) overly compressed the score distribution, discarding a non‑negligible fraction of genuine tops and squeezing the dynamic range of the final discriminant; additionally, the chosen ν = 4 for the t‑copula may still be too light‑tailed for the worst‑case resolution smearing, while the p_T scaling of the BDT weight might be mis‑calibrated for the intermediate‑p_T regime.
