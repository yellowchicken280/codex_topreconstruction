# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 726 Report

The modest but statistically significant rise to an efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 (≈ 62 %) can be traced to three deliberate design choices: (i) the introduction of pₜ‑dependent sigma floors (12 GeV for the top mass and 8 GeV for the W mass) that better track the true jet‐resolution as a function of kinematics, thereby preventing the likelihood terms from being artificially narrowed; (ii) replacing the hard dijet‑mass veto with a smooth spread‑penalty and an adaptive multiplier that only down‑weights events with simultaneously low BDT scores and large mass deviations, which rescued a sizable fraction of genuine, moderately‑boosted top candidates that were previously discarded; and (iii) the multiplicative combination of an exponentiated BDT, the top‑mass likelihood and the best‑W‑likelihood, which creates a non‑linear decision surface that sharpens signal–background separation while the tanh(pₜ/400) damping tames score inflation at extreme pₜ. These changes collectively lifted the efficiency toward the ≈ 65 % target, albeit at the cost of a small purity drop, most likely because the softer penalty still allows some background‑like configurations (especially low‑BDT, high‑mass‑deviation tails) to survive. The next step should therefore focus on regaining purity without eroding the gained efficiency: fine‑tune the sigma‑floor constants (or make them a continuous function of jet η and pile‑up), explore alternative penalty shapes (e.g. logistic or Gaussian weightings) and a more nuanced adaptive multiplier (perhaps based on a two‑dimensional BDT score vs. mass‑pull plane), incorporate per‑jet resolution estimates rather than static caps, and enrich the feature set (sub‑structure variables, refined b‑tag discriminants) before retraining the BDT. Parallel systematic studies—cross‑validation on independent simulated samples, validation in data control regions, and assessment of the tanh damping scale—will pinpoint whether the remaining impurity stems from over‑softening or from mismodelled backgrounds, guiding a final iteration that can reclaim some of the lost purity while preserving the ≈ 62 % efficiency.
