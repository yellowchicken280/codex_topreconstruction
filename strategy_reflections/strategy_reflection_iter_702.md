# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 702 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the pT‑dependent Gaussian mass window and the soft‑probability weighting of the three dijet masses succeeded in rescuing a sizable chunk of high‑pT tops that were formerly cut away by the rigid fixed‑width and hard asymmetry veto, confirming that smoothing the selection indeed improves signal acceptance; however, the overall efficiency still trails the 65 % goal because the steep logistic asymmetry function and the fixed exponent on the W‑mass probabilities, while less harsh than a binary cut, continue to down‑weight borderline candidates, and the purely multiplicative combination of the three terms creates a “bottleneck’’ where a single low‑valued component drags the final tag score down—additionally, the parametrisation of the pT‑dependent width may be overly conservative at the very highest pT where detector resolution degrades faster than modeled. The next step should therefore be a data‑driven re‑tuning of the width and asymmetry slopes, replacement of the fixed exponents with learnable weights (or a shallow neural‑net layer) that can adaptively balance the three mass probabilities, experimentation with an additive or hybrid scoring scheme to mitigate the bottleneck effect, and a retraining of the BDT on an enlarged high‑pT sample that also incorporates complementary sub‑structure variables (e.g., N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions) so that we can preserve background rejection while pushing the efficiency past the 0.65 threshold.
