# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 643 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 (still shy of the 0.64 goal) tells us that the physics‑aware soft‑penalty framework succeeded in loosening the overly brutal mass‑window veto and in rescuing some low‑pT tops, but the current parameterisation over‑compensates elsewhere, most likely by still penalising genuine configurations too harshly (the asymmetry term and the 30–130 GeV “mild” veto) and by diluting the discriminating power of the BDT through the sigmoid squeeze and an overly conservative pT‑dependent exponent that under‑weights the top‑mass kernel at low pT; the heavy‑tailed Cauchy kernel, while tolerant of resolution smearing, also admits a larger combinatorial background, eroding the net signal‑efficiency gain. The next step should be a systematic ablation and hyper‑parameter scan: (i) relax or re‑weight the asymmetry and range penalties (perhaps replace the hard 30–130 GeV window with a smoothly rising penalty); (ii) refine the σ_top(pT) functional form and the Cauchy scale to better match the actual detector resolution as a function of pT; (iii) test a softer, data‑driven mapping of the raw BDT output (e.g. a calibrated logistic rather than a plain sigmoid); (iv) explore alternative combination schemes (additive or weighted‑sum in log‑space) and allow the pT‑dependent exponent to be learned rather than fixed; and (v) incorporate per‑event mass‑resolution estimates so the kernels adapt event‑by‑event. Conducting these studies with Bayesian optimisation on a validation set will reveal whether the low‑pT tail can be fully recovered without sacrificing background rejection, ultimately pushing the efficiency past the 0.64 target.
