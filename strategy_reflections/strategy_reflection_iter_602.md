# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 602 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % efficiency shows that the pₜ‑dependent W‑mass window and the milder sigmoid successfully rescued a fraction of the low‑pₜ tops that the original aggressive veto and steep warping erased, confirming that the physics‑driven “soft‑candidate” rescue works in principle, but the multiplicative assembly of the Gaussian mass‑consistency weights, the Student‑t χ² penalty, and the pₜ‑modulated BDT exponent still over‑suppresses signal when resolution fluctuations push a candidate slightly out of the narrow Gaussian kernels or generate a modest χ² outlier; in practice the heavy‑tailed ν≈2.2 penalty does not fully compensate for detector smearing, and the exponent range 0.7→1.0 may be too conservative for the softest tops. To push toward the 64–65 % goal we should broaden the Gaussian kernels (or replace them with adaptive kernel densities), re‑tune ν to a lighter tail (e.g. ν≈1.5–1.8), and allow a more aggressive low‑pₜ exponent (perhaps down to 0.55) while preserving high‑pₜ stability; exploring an additive rather than strictly multiplicative combination could also prevent cascading down‑weighting, and a small grid search or Bayesian optimisation on a validation set—especially focusing on low‑pₜ control regions—would reveal the optimal k≈5–6, offset≈0.35–0.45 for the sigmoid. Finally, integrating these physics‑motivated features directly into the BDT training (or a shallow neural net) would let the classifier learn the best weighting rather than imposing it post‑hoc, likely delivering the remaining efficiency boost needed.
