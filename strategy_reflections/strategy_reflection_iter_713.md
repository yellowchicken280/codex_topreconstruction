# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 713 Report

The smooth‑penalty approach of strategy_v713 clearly succeeded in rescuing a subset of mis‑measured top candidates—evidenced by a modest rise in signal efficiency and retained background rejection—but the overall efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.0152 still falls short of the 0.64 goal, suggesting that the new functional forms are still over‑penalising genuine signal or under‑utilising the BDT power; the tanh‑shaped χ² term, while gentle near the nominal masses, becomes harsh beyond roughly 2σ and trims away tops with realistic mass fluctuations, the Gaussian‑like asymmetry term’s width appears too narrow to accommodate the natural dijet mass spread, and the sigmoid pT weight, tuned to suppress the high‑pT combinatorial tail, inadvertently down‑weights a non‑negligible portion of the abundant low‑to‑moderate‑pT signal; moreover, raising the raw BDT score to α = 0.6 may have dulled its discriminating strength, allowing the penalty factors to dominate the decision and letting more background survive. The next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter optimisation—varying the tanh steepness and offset, the asymmetry σ, the sigmoid midpoint and slope, and the exponent α—using a blinded validation set and cross‑validation to map efficiency versus background rejection as a function of top pT and χ²; exploring alternative combinations (e.g., a log‑sum or learned weighting via a shallow neural net) could prevent excessive suppression of moderate‑score events, and adding complementary observables such as ΔR(b,W) or b‑tag discriminants into the penalty framework may further sharpen the decision surface, ultimately guiding a refined version that pushes the efficiency past the 64 % barrier without compromising purity.
