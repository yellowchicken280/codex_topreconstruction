# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 672 Report

The new smooth‑likelihood approach clearly helped mitigate the hard‑cut losses that plagued earlier versions—by using Gaussian mass terms, a geometric‑mean W‑candidate probability, and a soft asymmetry penalty, genuine top‑quark combos that were slightly smeared by detector resolution are now retained, and the sigmoid \(p_T\) weighting plus the power‑enhanced BDT do give a modest boost to confident signal candidates; however, the overall signal efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 still falls short of the targeted 64 % ceiling, indicating that the added non‑linearity is also suppressing a subset of true tops, likely those in the very high‑\(p_T\) tail where the sigmoid and exponent over‑penalise the already‑strong raw BDT score and the tanh compression flattens useful discrimination.  The next step should therefore be a focused hyper‑parameter scan: tighten the Gaussian widths to better match the actual mass resolution, relax the asymmetry weight or make it \(p_T\)‑dependent, adjust the sigmoid centre and steepness to preserve more ultra‑boosted tops, and explore exponents < 1 or a piecewise power to avoid over‑amplifying borderline events; simultaneously, re‑evaluate the tanh scaling (or replace it with a calibrated logistic) and validate each tweak on separate high‑ and low‑\(p_T\) control samples to pinpoint where signal loss remains highest, after which one can decide whether further refinements (e.g. adding sub‑jet b‑tag information or a secondary neural‑network combiner) are warranted.
