# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 696 Report

The modest yet statistically‑significant rise to a 61.6 % signal efficiency ( ± 1.5 % ) shows that softening the hard cut‑offs and embedding physics‑motivated, continuous weighting functions paid off: widening the Student‑t core at low pₜ prevented the brutal loss of intermediate‑pₜ tops, the sigmoid boost gently rewarded truly boosted candidates without creating a hard “step’’ in the response, and the logistic soft‑veto kept the turn‑on around 130 GeV smooth enough to rescue genuine tops while still pruning the bulk of low‑mass background; the exponential term for the dijet‑mass proximity to m_W and the Gaussian‑like spread penalty further nudged the classifier toward physically plausible top shapes. The gain is clearly driven by the smoother landscape that the BDT can exploit, but the trade‑off is a slightly more permissive background acceptance that must be quantified (the current multi‑stage veto may be too lax in the soft‑veto region, and the exponential weight may over‑emphasise marginal W‑mass peaks). The next step is therefore a systematic scan of the sigmoid, logistic, exponential and Gaussian hyper‑parameters (e.g. turning‑point location, steepness, width) using a signal‑significance metric (S/√(S+B)) rather than raw efficiency, complemented by validation on orthogonal control regions and a ROC‑curve comparison to the previous hard‑penalty design; if background leakage proves problematic, we can re‑introduce a mild hard pₜ gate or a calibrated, data‑driven mass‑spread penalty while preserving the smooth core that rescued the lost tops.
