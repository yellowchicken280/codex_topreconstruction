# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 699 Report

The continuous‐weighting idea behind strategy_v708 did indeed soften the hard cuts and rescued a modest number of genuine top candidates that were previously thrown away, but the net efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 still falls short of the 0.64 target, suggesting that the gains from the Gaussian mass‑likelihoods and the high‑pT logistic boost were largely cancelled by an overly aggressive asymmetry penalty and a fairly steep soft‑window fall‑off; in practice the chosen σ values for the top‑ and W‑mass Gaussians appear too narrow for the true detector resolution, and the asymmetry term still down‑weights events with realistic QCD radiation. To push the efficiency higher we should re‑tune the hyper‑parameters—broaden the mass‑Gaussian widths to match the measured resolutions, soften or reshaped the asymmetry penalty, and adjust the logistic boost exponent—while also exploring more flexible ways to combine the individual factors (e.g., a small neural‑network or a learned multivariate score) and retraining the BDT with these continuous weights as additional features, so the algorithm can learn the optimal balance between signal acceptance and background rejection.
