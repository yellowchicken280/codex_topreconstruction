# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 712 Report

The modest gain of 0.616 ± 0.015 compared with the 0.64 target shows that, while the added W‑mass average, explicit top‑mass likelihood and asymmetry penalty sharpened background rejection, the way they were combined ended up being overly punitive for genuine top candidates: the hard‑core 0.1 veto for any dijet mass beyond 2 σ and the steep exponential asymmetry term stripped away a sizable fraction of partially‑mis‑measured but still signal‑like events, and the pT‑dependent sigmoid discarded lower‑pT tops that constitute a non‑negligible part of the spectrum; moreover, the multiplicative form together with the fractional‑power weighting amplified the impact of any single low‑scoring term. To move forward, I would first soften the veto (e.g. replace the 0.1 factor with a smooth tanh‑based penalty) and relax the asymmetry exponent, then re‑optimize the σ thresholds and the sigmoid steepness on an independent validation set; next, I would explore additive (log‑sum) combination of the normalized components rather than a pure product, and perform a systematic scan of the fractional powers (or let a simple gradient‑based optimizer find optimal exponents). Finally, I would augment the discriminant with additional orthogonal observables such as b‑tag scores or jet‑substructure variables, and use a small BDT or logistic‑regression layer to learn the optimal weighting, thereby preserving the physical insight of the handcrafted terms while regaining the signal efficiency needed to breach the 0.64 barrier.
