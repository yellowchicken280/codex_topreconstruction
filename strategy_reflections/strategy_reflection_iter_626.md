# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 626 Report

The modest but statistically significant jump to a 61.6 % tagging efficiency (≈+0.02 absolute over the baseline) shows that the “soft‑window” philosophy of strategy_v626 is indeed paying off: by discarding sub‑30 GeV dijets the classifier is protected from pathological three‑prong kinematics, while the sigmoidal reshaping of the dijet‑mass, w_dev, w_spread and mass_balance observables smooths out the hard cut‑offs that crippleed the classic tagger at high boost; the Gaussian top‑mass weight and the pT‑dependent tanh scaling then rescue those slightly mis‑reconstructed tops where resolution degrades, giving the BDT a gentle, pT‑aware boost.  The remaining ≈1.5 % uncertainty and a slight dip in background rejection suggest that the aggressive tanh^1.2 amplification may be over‑accentuating statistical fluctuations, especially in the low‑pT regime where the pT‑factor is small.  Going forward the most promising levers are (i) fine‑tuning the tanh exponent and the pT‑scale (e.g. exploring a data‑driven pT‑dependent “β” parameter), (ii) adding complementary substructure inputs such as N‑subjettiness ratios or energy‑correlation functions to further disentangle QCD splittings from true three‑prong decays, and (iii) validating the pipeline on independent high‑pT samples and with cross‑validation to guard against over‑training; a brief hyper‑parameter sweep and the inclusion of a regularisation term in the BDT loss should quickly reveal whether the observed gain can be solidified into a robust ~0.5 %–1 % efficiency improvement without sacrificing purity.
