# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 666 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency of strategy_NLCombo_v666 shows that the smooth, physics‑inspired weighting scheme succeeds in recapturing a sizable fraction of signal that binary cuts throw away—particularly in the high‑pT regime where the logistic pT term boosts the score and the tanh‑shaped top‑mass plateau tolerates modest mass smearing without harshly discarding events, while the max‑pull W‑boson term ensures the most “W‑like’’ dijet pair dominates the weight rather than being diluted by poorer combinations; simultaneously, the exponential variance penalty and the compressed‑tanh BDT factor keep the score from exploding on outliers, preserving background rejection. Nonetheless, the modest gain comes with possible drawbacks: the fixed functional forms (e.g. the tanh exponent, logistic steepness, exponential variance scale) were chosen heuristically and may not be optimal across the full pT spectrum, the max‑pull approach can be vulnerable to occasional accidental high pulls in background, and compressing the BDT output could blunt discrimination for borderline cases, potentially inflating false‑positive rates. The next step should therefore be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (tuning the tanh exponent, logistic plateau, variance‑scale and BDT compression) combined with a detailed ROC and pT‑binned performance study on an independent validation set and on data‑driven background estimates; exploring alternatives such as a soft‑max combination of the three dijet pulls, adding sub‑structure observables (e.g. N‑subjettiness or energy‑correlation functions), and possibly replacing the handcrafted non‑linear composition with a calibrated shallow neural network will help test whether the observed efficiency gain is robust or can be further improved without sacrificing background suppression.
