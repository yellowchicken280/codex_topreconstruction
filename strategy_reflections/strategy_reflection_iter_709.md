# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 709 Report

The modest 61.6 % signal efficiency indicates that, while the physics‑driven ingredients of strategy_v709 succeeded in taming pathological jet pairings and preserving a sharp W‑mass peak, the resulting score was still too conservative to reach the projected 64 % goal – most of the shortfall appears to stem from the two‑stage veto and the strong asymmetry penalty, which together excise a non‑trivial fraction of genuine, but slightly mis‑measured, top candidates (especially at intermediate pT where the σ‑scaling is largest); the softened BDT exponent (γ≈0.45) also under‑weights high‑score events, further suppressing borderline tops, while the tanh boost may be insufficiently aggressive for the most boosted regime. To close the gap we should systematically re‑optimize the veto thresholds (e.g. relax the 2σ/2.5σ cut or replace the hard cut with a smoother penalty), explore a gentler asymmetry exponent (perhaps exp(‑k·asym²) with k<5), and retune γ to balance background rejection against efficiency (a scan around 0.55–0.6). Adding complementary sub‑structure observables (τ₃/τ₂, soft‑drop mass) into the combined_score or replacing the multiplicative form with a calibrated logistic‐regression blend could capture residual discriminating power, and a dedicated pT‑binned cross‑validation would reveal whether the current σ‑scaling accurately reflects resolution across the spectrum. Iterating these refinements should push the efficiency above the 64 % target while retaining the robust background suppression achieved so far.
