# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 660 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % ± 1.5 % shows that loosening the hard vetoes and letting the mass resolutions grow with pT did recover many genuine tops that were previously discarded, confirming that the original fixed‑window cuts were the dominant source of inefficiency; however, we fell short of the 64 % goal because the remaining penalisation mechanisms are still too aggressive for a sizable fraction of signal – the linear σ ∝ pT scaling likely over‑estimates the resolution at very high boost, the sigmoid boost caps the contribution of intermediate‑pT candidates (where most of the signal lives) at only ~0.5, and the binary 3σ veto still removes events in the tails of the Gaussian likelihoods, while the 0.55 exponent on the BDT score continues to dominate the overall weight, limiting the impact of the physics‑based terms.  The next step should be a focused re‑tuning: replace the linear σ(pT) with a slower‑rising (e.g. sqrt or piecewise) function calibrated on data‑driven resolution studies, soften the sigmoid (or shift its midpoint) to give more weight to the 200–300 GeV region, replace the hard 3σ veto with a continuous penalty (e.g. exp(−Δ²/2σ²)) and explore a modest reduction of the BDT exponent (e.g. 0.45) to let the likelihood components contribute more, while simultaneously performing a small hyper‑parameter scan (or Bayesian optimisation) on all exponents and the asymmetry‑penalty strength; an ablation study after these adjustments will reveal which change delivers the largest efficiency uplift without sacrificing the background rejection already achieved.
