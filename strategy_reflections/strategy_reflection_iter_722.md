# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 722 Report

The modest jump to ε = 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the new ingredients of strategy_v722 are indeed moving the reconstruction in the right direction – the logarithmic growth of σ_top and σ_W successfully widens the Gaussian acceptance for truly boosted tops, the soft‑max of the three dijet‑mass scores smooths the response and preserves the most W‑like pairing without harsh cut‑offs, and the pT‑scaled tanh asymmetry term and two‑stage veto efficiently prune low‑pT, combinatorial triplets while leaving borderline high‑pT candidates untouched; moreover, merging the original BDT score with the log‑likelihood lets the physics‑driven term dominate where it is most reliable. However, the overall efficiency still falls short of the > 0.64 goal, indicating that residual over‑penalisation of medium‑pT signal (perhaps from an overly aggressive asymmetry weight or the hard low‑pT veto) and/or an insufficiently flexible modeling of the top‑mass tail at extreme boosts are still limiting performance. The next iteration should therefore (i) re‑optimize the pT‑dependence of σ_top (and σ_W) – for example by allowing a mild power‑law term on top of the logarithm to capture the genuine broadening at the highest pT, (ii) fine‑tune the sigmoid steepness and asymmetry scaling constants with a dedicated grid search or Bayesian optimizer, (iii) replace the fixed linear combination of BDT and log‑likelihood with a learnable meta‑learner (e.g. a shallow neural net) that can adapt the relative weight per‑event, (iv) augment the kinematic likelihood with sub‑structure observables such as n‑subjettiness or energy‑correlation functions that are especially discriminating for highly collimated tops, and (v) validate the revised veto thresholds on a mixed‑pT validation set to ensure that borderline signal is retained while background remains suppressed. By iterating on these points the strategy should be able to push the reconstruction efficiency past the 64 % barrier without inflating the background rate.
