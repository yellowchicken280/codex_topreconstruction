# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 724 Report

The modest gain of 61.6 % ± 1.5 % shows that the stage‑wise veto and dynamic‐sigma mass windows did help soften the overly punitive product of the earlier scheme—borderline triplets that were previously discarded now contribute to the score, and the weighted geometric mean lets a strong BDT response rescue modest mass mismatches—yet the overall efficiency still falls short of the 64 % goal because the loosening also admits a larger fraction of badly‑matched or combinatorial backgrounds, which dilutes the true‑top purity and forces the hyperbolic‑tangent pT‑saturation and asymmetry penalty to over‑compensate for high‑energy tails; in particular the 0.4/0.3/0.2 exponent balance may give too much leverage to the BDT at the expense of the mass likelihoods, and the logarithmic sigma growth can become overly generous at high pT, eroding discrimination.
