# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 658 Report

The 61.6 % efficiency of strategy_v658 shows that the physics‑driven ingredients did improve the handling of moderately‑boosted tops—especially the dynamic W‑mass width and the Gaussian‑likelihood + geometric‑mean rescue—yet the overall gain fell short of the 64 % target because the loosened pₜ‑weight and the variance‑based balance term over‑relaxed the penalty for combinatorial triplets in the mid‑pₜ region, allowing too many background‑like configurations to survive, while the final gw_geo veto sometimes discarded genuine candidates whose three dijet masses were spread by final‑state radiation. Moreover, the log‑space product can become dominated by a single term, diminishing the intended compensatory effect, and the fixed slopes and σ‑values of the dynamic width and Gaussian PDFs were likely sub‑optimal. The next step should be a focused hyper‑parameter scan (slope of σ_w, Gaussian σ’s, logistic steepness, variance‑term weight) possibly split into low‑, intermediate‑, and high‑pₜ regimes, combined with a modest multivariate layer (e.g. a shallow BDT) to learn the optimal non‑linear combination; adding complementary discriminants such as b‑tag scores, N‑subjettiness or soft‑drop mass, and testing a robust alternative to the geometric mean (e.g. max‑likelihood) would further tighten the selection while preserving boosted‑top efficiency.
