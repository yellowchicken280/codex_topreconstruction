# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 597 Report

The modest drop from the baseline 64 % to 61.6 % ± 1.5 % suggests that while the physics‑driven “mass_prob” term did introduce a sensible top‑mass constraint, its rigid χ²‑like penalty over‑suppressed genuine top candidates whose reconstructed masses were smeared by jet‑energy resolution or by additional radiation, and the pT‑dependent weighting biased the selection toward high‑pT tops at the expense of the bulk of the sample; moreover, the steep logistic transformation of the original BDT score, although intended to sharpen signal‑background separation, appears to have amplified statistical fluctuations in the raw BDT output and inadvertently diminished the classifier’s intrinsic discrimination power when combined multiplicatively with the new factors. To recover or surpass the original efficiency we should (i) re‑tune the relative weight of the χ² term (perhaps using a softer, resolution‑aware likelihood rather than a hard quadratic penalty), (ii) replace the fixed pT factor with a smoothly varying function learned from data, (iii) explore a joint training where the mass‑constraint information is fed back into the BDT (or a deep‑learning tagger) rather than appended post‑hoc, and (iv) perform an exhaustive hyper‑parameter scan (including the logistic slope) validated on independent control samples to ensure the added physics terms improve true‑top reconstruction without sacrificing the robust pattern‑recognition learned by the original model.
