# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 745 Report

The modest 61.6 % ± 1.5 % efficiency shows that the physics‑driven factorisation in strategy_v752 succeeded in sharpening the signal peak but also over‑constrained the genuine top candidates – the product of a steep β‑power boost of the raw BDT, narrow Gaussian pulls (σ≈15 GeV for mₜ and σ≈10 GeV for m_W), a relatively aggressive p_T/(p_T + p₀)^γ term, and the min/max dijet‑mass‑ratio penalty together carved out a very tight region of phase space, so many real tops with modest resolution smearing or slight energy asymmetry were vetoed by the hard mass‑window cut. In particular, the σ values may be too narrow for the detector resolution of the current sample, and the exponent β>1 compresses low‑score events that could still be rescued by the other weights; the ratio penalty likewise penalizes legitimate W‑decay jets that suffer occasional radiation‑induced imbalance. To improve, I would first relax the Gaussian widths (or replace them with calibrated likelihood PDFs), re‑tune β, γ and p₀ on a validation set to balance high‑p_T gain against low‑p_T loss, and soften the min/max penalty (e.g. using a smooth function rather than a hard factor). An alternative is to let a multivariate optimizer learn the optimal combination of these descriptors instead of a simple product, or to add a complementary top‑substructure variable (N‑subjettiness, H_T, etc.) to recover events that fail one of the strict cuts. Finally, expanding the training sample will reduce the statistical uncertainty and allow a more robust cross‑validation of the tuned parameters, aiming to push the efficiency past the 64 % plateau without sacrificing background rejection.
