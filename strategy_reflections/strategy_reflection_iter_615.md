# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 615 Report

The strategy v615 clearly succeeded in rescuing many genuine top candidates that were lost to the previous hard 3σ vetoes, as the smooth χ²‑based mass likelihood with pT‑dependent resolutions and the softened exponent (0.9) together restored efficiency from the low‑50 % range to 0.616 ± 0.015, demonstrating that continuous weighting and the sigmoid‑calibrated BDT probability better accommodate detector smearing, especially for moderate‑pT tops; however, the gain still falls short of the 65 % goal, indicating that the current tuning remains overly punitive for a subset of acceptable events— the 2σ “boost” is perhaps too narrow, the sigmoid centred at 150 GeV may under‑weight tops just below this scale, and a fixed exponent does not adapt to the varying resolution across pT. To push the efficiency higher we should re‑optimise the sigmoid centre and slope (or replace it with a pT‑dependent functional form), explore a dynamic exponent that scales with the estimated mass resolution, broaden the generous 2σ window, and augment the likelihood with additional discriminants such as N‑subjettiness, soft‑drop mass, or energy‑correlation functions; a systematic grid‑scan or Bayesian optimisation on a validation sample, followed by thorough background‑leakage studies, will reveal whether these refinements can lift the efficiency into the 64–65 % regime without sacrificing purity.
