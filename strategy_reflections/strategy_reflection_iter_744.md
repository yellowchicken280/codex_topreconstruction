# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 744 Report

The new strategy clearly softened the overly harsh penalties of the old version – the pT‑dependent resolution functions let low‑pT tops survive, the quadratic‑mean (p = 2) combination reduced the “one‑weak‑weight kills everything” effect, and the asymmetry term helped prune combinatorial background, yielding a smoother score and a modest bump in efficiency to 0.616 ± 0.015; however, this is still below the 0.65 target, suggesting that the added asymmetry penalty and the logistic pT boost may be over‑penalising genuine signal (especially mildly asymmetric W candidates or intermediate‑pT tops) and that raising the BDT to the power α = 0.9 has flattened its discriminating power too much. The next step should be a focused hyper‑parameter optimisation: scan the power‑mean exponent (e.g. p ∈ [1.5, 3]), adjust the strength and functional form of the asymmetry term, try a slightly larger α or a piece‑wise scaling of the BDT output, and refine the pT‑dependent resolution functions using data‑driven calibration; replacing the fixed 40 GeV W‑mass veto with a dynamic window tied to the mass uncertainty, or even employing a small neural‑network combiner for the physics weights, could further recover lost signal while keeping background rejection high, ultimately pushing the efficiency past the 65 % goal.
