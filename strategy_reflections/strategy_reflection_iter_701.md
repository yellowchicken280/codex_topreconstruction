# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 701 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % shows that smoothing the harsh binary cuts did make the tagger more forgiving – the pT‑dependent top‑mass Gaussian and logistic uplift indeed rescued a handful of mid‑pT candidates that the previous >2σ veto would have thrown away – but the overall design still over‑penalises the bulk of the sample, mainly through an overly aggressive quartic asymmetry term and a still‑tight final asymmetry veto (>35 GeV) that trims away many “border‑line” signal tops whose W‑mass spread is inflated by detector smearing or pile‑up; additionally, the pT‑dependent σ may be underestimated at high pT, causing the Gaussian to down‑weight legitimate high‑pT tops, and the softened BDT exponent, while reducing fluctuations, also dilutes the discriminating power and lets more background survive, thus limiting the net efficiency gain. The next step is to retune the functional forms and thresholds: calibrate the σ(pT) curve on data‑driven resolution studies, explore a gentler asymmetry penalty (e.g. a cubic or piecewise linear form) and relax the hard veto to a softer multivariate decision, and fine‑tune the logistic boost and BDT exponent via a systematic grid or Bayesian optimization while monitoring background rejection; incorporating additional substructure observables (τ3/τ2, girth, etc.) or moving to a deeper classifier that learns optimal weightings could also help push the efficiency past the 64 % target without sacrificing purity.
