# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 607 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 shows that the soft two‑out‑of‑three logic and the p_T‑dependent Cauchy width did indeed soften the harsh vetoes that crippled the earlier AND‑style selector, allowing a number of low‑p_T tops that would have been discarded to survive, yet the gain fell short of the 0.64 target because the dynamic resolution model is still too aggressive for the most poorly measured W‑pairings and the sigmoid p_T weight remains overly penalising for jets near the boost threshold, causing the top‑2 averaging to pick the same two strong weights (often w_top and w_BDT) while w_pair remains suppressed enough to drag the average down; in addition, the logistic mapping of the raw BDT score may be compressing the tail of genuinely signal‑like candidates, limiting the final weight. The next iteration should therefore (i) re‑calibrate σ_w(p_T) and σ_top(p_T) using the full residual distribution of MC truth to capture the true scaling of mass resolution, (ii) experiment with a softer combination rule such as a weighted‑sum or softmax of the three physics weights rather than a strict top‑2 average, (iii) tune the p_T sigmoid’s midpoint and steepness to better reflect the actual acceptance curve, and (iv) explore adding a calibrated b‑tag probability or jet‑substructure discriminator directly into the physics weight so that the BDT contribution is not the sole driver of the final score; a brief hyper‑parameter scan over these knobs on a validation set will reveal whether a modest reshaping can push the efficiency past the 0.64 wall without sacrificing background rejection.
