# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 651 Report

The modest gain in signal efficiency achieved by strategy_v651 shows that separating the four physically motivated discriminants and mapping them with non‑linear functions does preserve the BDT’s raw power while alleviating the premature pT suppression and overly tight W‑mass veto of earlier versions, but the resulting 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency—still below the 0.64 target—reveals that the current weighting and functional forms are not fully exploiting the complementary information: the steep sigmoid (≈ 10) compresses most BDT scores, the geometric‑mean aggregation penalises any single low‑valued term (e.g. a modest pT or a slightly off‑mass dijet) disproportionately, and the 0.20 share given to the W‑mass term may be too small to pull down combinatorial background, while focusing only on the best dijet candidate can still discard genuine tops when the true W‑pair is not the top‑ranked combination. To push the efficiency past the barrier we should flatten the BDT sigmoid (e.g. use a logistic with a gentler slope or a calibrated probability), experiment with an additive or hybrid weighted‑sum instead of a pure geometric mean, increase the W‑mass contribution or introduce a secondary‑candidate term with a softer Gaussian penalty, retune the log‑space weights by scanning the three‑dimensional simplex (e.g. via Bayesian optimisation), and consider augmenting the set with orthogonal variables such as b‑tag scores or angular observables; a systematic hyper‑parameter scan on a larger validation sample will then reveal which combination yields the desired efficiency uplift without raising the fake‑top rate.
