# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 628 Report

The measured efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 for strategy B6585 shows that the “soft‑cut” philosophy did indeed rescue a sizable fraction of moderate‑boost top candidates that were previously lost to the rigid Gaussian window and hard balance term, confirming that the exponential inflation of σ_top and σ_W at low p_T and the Gaussian penalties turn binary vetoes into smooth weightings, while the geometric‑mean of the three W‑candidate probabilities retains the necessary three‑jet W‑mass consistency; however, the modest gain relative to the baseline also hints at new limitations – the tanh(p_T) boost may be over‑damping very high‑p_T tops, the logistic tail still discards events with substantial top‑mass deviations rather than merely down‑weighting them, and the broadened resolution windows inevitably admit more background, eroding purity. The next step should therefore focus on a systematic retuning of the functional forms: replace the simple exponential σ(p_T) growth with a data‑driven resolution model (e.g. σ(p_T)=σ₀[1+α log(1+p_T/p₀)]), adjust the tanh scale and steepness to allow a gentler transition into the boosted regime, explore alternative combination schemes such as a weighted product or a harmonic mean of the W‑probabilities (to penalize any single badly‑reconstructed W more strongly), and fine‑tune the logistic tail’s midpoint and width using a validation sample that balances efficiency against background rejection (e.g. ROC‑curve optimisation). Adding a per‑event estimate of the jet‑energy resolution (from in‑situ calibration or MC truth) could further tailor the Gaussian widths, while a small multivariate classifier trained on the residuals of the mass terms could provide an extra discriminant without discarding the transparent physics‑motivated structure of the current scheme. A focused hyper‑parameter scan (or Bayesian optimisation) around these adjustments, followed by a full performance study of both efficiency and purity, will reveal whether the softened cuts can be tightened just enough to preserve the newly gained acceptance while regaining the background suppression lost in the first iteration.
