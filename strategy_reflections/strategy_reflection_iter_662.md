# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 662 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the physics‑driven, non‑linear combination captured many of the missing top‑quark configurations that the previous linear σ∝p_T and raw‑BDT weighting discarded, especially in the intermediate‑boost regime where the √p_T‑scaled mass resolution and the tanh‑bounded BDT term correctly softened the influence of outliers; however, the overall efficiency still fell short of the 65 % goal because the χ²‑style W‑mass term and the conditional high‑p_T veto together over‑penalise a non‑trivial fraction of genuine signal events, particularly the very‑boosted tops where even a 30 GeV deviation is common due to jet‑substructure effects, and the dijet‑mass‑balance penalty may be too aggressive for asymmetric but still correct pairings. The next iteration should therefore (i) retune the σ_w(p_T) and top_term width functions to allow a slightly broader tolerance at the highest p_T, (ii) replace the hard 30 GeV cut with a smoothly decreasing weight (e.g. a Gaussian with a p_T‑dependent sigma), (iii) experiment with a softer tanh scaling or a calibrated sigmoid to preserve BDT discrimination while avoiding saturation, (iv) introduce a jet‑substructure variable (e.g. N‑subjettiness or soft‑drop mass) as an additional, less‑correlated term, and (v) perform a data‑driven optimisation of the relative product weights using a grid‑search or Bayesian optimiser on a validation set. These refinements should recover the remaining ~3 % efficiency gap without inflating the background rate.
