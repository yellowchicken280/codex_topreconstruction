# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 758 Report

The 0.616±0.015 efficiency of strategy_B_65_boost shows that the physics‑driven refinements – picking the dijet closest to the true W mass, letting σ_W and σ_top grow with the triplet p_T, and applying a p_T‑dependent exponent to the raw BDT – indeed rescued many genuine tops that the earlier likelihood‑based approach had over‑penalised, especially in the 300–600 GeV window where the logistic p_T weight and the mild asymmetry‑penalty kept the signal alive; however, the overall target of >64 % was not reached, suggesting that the dynamic smearing and weighting schemes are still too aggressive for a sizable fraction of events, probably mis‑modelling the resolution broadening at intermediate p_T and under‑weighting low‑to‑moderate‑p_T candidates, while the fast veto may be discarding borderline but recoverable topologies. Moving forward, we should (i) recalibrate the σ(p_T) functions with data‑driven jet‑mass resolution studies to avoid over‑smearing, (ii) scan the exponent‑vs‑p_T functional form (e.g. piecewise linear or spline) to preserve high‑p_T discrimination without sacrificing mid‑range recall, (iii) fine‑tune the asymmetry‑penalty coefficient to be even more forgiving when the second‑best dijet is close to the W mass, (iv) enrich the feature set with jet‑substructure observables (τ_21, groomed mass, energy‑correlation functions) that can further distinguish true W candidates from combinatorial pairs, and (v) replace the binary multistage veto with a secondary gradient‑boosted classifier that learns a softer decision boundary. A systematic cross‑validation on independent MC samples (and ultimately on control data) will quantify any trade‑off between efficiency and background rejection, allowing us to iteratively push the efficiency past the 64 % benchmark while keeping the fake‑top rate under control.
