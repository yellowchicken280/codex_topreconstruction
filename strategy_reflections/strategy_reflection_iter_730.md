# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 730 Report

The new “strategy_B_v650” succeeded in softening the harsh pT‑turn‑on that had been starving semi‑boosted tops, as evidenced by a modest uptick in the low‑pT region and a clean rescue of candidates in the 150–190 GeV window; however, the overall tagging efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.0152 still fell short of the 0.64 target, mainly because the two‑stage sigmoid’s fall‑off above ≈350 GeV, together with the pT‑dependent Gaussian width (σ = 15 GeV + 0.02·pT), over‑penalised high‑pT tops and allowed more badly‑reconstructed jets to survive, while the 0.68 exponent on the raw BDT score compressed its discriminating power more than anticipated. In hindsight the rescue factor (~7 %) and the final 0.93 exponent were too modest to compensate for the loss of separation at high pT, and the fixed σ scaling may be too aggressive; next steps should therefore focus on re‑optimising the sigmoid breakpoints (e.g. shifting the upper knee to ~400 GeV and softening its slope), reducing the σ growth term (perhaps σ = 15 GeV + 0.01·pT), loosening the top‑mass window or increasing the rescue weight, and exploring a less aggressive BDT compression (exponent ≈0.80) or a pT‑aware BDT trained in separate pT slices. Adding complementary sub‑structure variables (τ₃₂, D₂) and performing a systematic hyper‑parameter sweep—validated on both signal efficiency and background mistag rate—will help gauge whether these refinements can push the efficiency past the 64 % mark without sacrificing background rejection.
