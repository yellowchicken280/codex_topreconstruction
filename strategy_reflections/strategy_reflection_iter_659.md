# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 659 Report

The modest 61.6 % signal efficiency of strategy_v659 shows that, while the physics‑driven ingredients (pT‑dependent W‑mass window, Gaussian likelihoods for the three dijet masses, a variance‑penalty, a top‑mass Gaussian term and a logistic pT‑mass boost) succeeded in tightening background rejection, they also over‑constrained genuine top candidates—most notably the variance‑penalty and the two‑stage veto, which together excise a sizable fraction of correctly reconstructed triplets whose sub‑jet masses are slightly asymmetric or whose W‑mass windows drift beyond the hard cut‑offs; the geometric‑mean combination further dampens the influence of a single well‑measured W, so that any modest mis‑measurement drags the whole score down. To push the efficiency past the 64 % target we should (i) re‑tune the exponents (α,β,γ,δ) and perhaps replace the hard vetoes with softer, probabilistic penalties, (ii) broaden the allowed W‑mass window scaling (or make its pT dependence learnable) to accommodate ISR/FSR effects, (iii) test alternative aggregation schemes (e.g. weighted arithmetic mean or a learned linear combination) that preserve the contribution of a strong W candidate, and (iv) augment the handcrafted score with a modest BDT or shallow neural‑network that can capture residual correlations among sub‑jet kinematics; a systematic grid‑search or Bayesian optimisation on a separate validation set will quantify the trade‑off between background rejection and signal acceptance and guide the next iteration of the tagger.
