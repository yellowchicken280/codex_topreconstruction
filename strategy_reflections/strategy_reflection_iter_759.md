# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 759 Report

The 61.6 % efficiency achieved by strategy_nonlinear_ptmass_v01 shows that the physically‑motivated discriminants—Δ_W for explicit W‑mass consistency, the RMS of the three dijet masses to capture the collimation of a genuine three‑body top decay, and a pT‑dependent σ_top calibrated with a logarithmic growth—indeed rescued many tops that were over‑penalised by the earlier linear σ(pT) and simple pT‑weighted BDT, while the modest non‑linear power (1.2) and logistic pT factor gave a gentle boost to low‑pT candidates and the second‑stage veto safely removed pathological dijet masses; however, the multiplicative combination with exponential damping proved too aggressive in regions where the RMS or Δ_W fluctuated near threshold, and the fixed exponent and veto cut values likely over‑suppress borderline signal events, leaving the overall efficiency still shy of the 64 % goal. The next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan: tune the RMS and Δ_W tolerance, explore a softer (e.g. additive or weighted‑sum) combination rather than pure multiplication, adjust the exponent and logistic pT shape, and refine σ_top using data‑driven calibration (e.g. in‑situ W‑mass peaks) to reduce residual smearing; simultaneously, augment the feature set with jet‑substructure observables (τ₃/τ₂, ECFs) or a secondary “veto‑BDT” trained on the outliers, and validate the revised model across multiple MC tunes and control‑region data to ensure robustness before committing to the next iteration.
