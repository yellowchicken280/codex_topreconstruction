# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 761 Report

The modest uplift to 0.616 ± 0.015 reflects that many of the new ingredients did succeed in rescuing low‑\(p_{\rm T}\) candidates and in softening the hard cuts that previously ate genuine tops, but the combined non‑linear weighting still over‑penalises a sizeable fraction of borderline events—likely because the logistic \(p_{\rm T}\) weight, the 1.2 exponent, and the two lightweight vetoes remain too aggressive for moderate‑\(p_{\rm T}\) tops, and the mixed Gaussian‑Cauchy kernel and pt‑dependent mass resolution were not perfectly tuned to the true detector tails, causing a loss of discriminating power after the sigmoid compression of the BDT score. To bridge the remaining 2–3 % gap, the next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (e.g. varying the logistic slope, exponent, veto thresholds, and kernel mixing fractions) together with a data‑driven calibration of the pt‑dependent resolution, while also enriching the feature set with jet‑substructure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions) and allowing a more flexible meta‑classifier—perhaps a second‑level gradient‑boosted or shallow neural network—to learn the optimal combination of the probabilistic terms rather than imposing a handcrafted functional form; expanding the validation sample will also shrink the statistical uncertainty and clarify whether the residual shortfall is systematic or merely a statistical fluctuation.
