# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 614 Report

Although strategy_v652 managed to tighten the mass‐constraint terms and the pₜ‑dependent width of the top‑mass Gaussian, thereby sharpening the response for truly boosted tops, the overall efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015 still fell short of the 64 % goal, likely because the aggressive logistic pₜ weighting, the pₜ‑scaled BDT exponent, and the steep non‑linear powers (1.2 for the top‑mass Gaussian and 1.5 for the W‑mass Gaussian) over‑penalised legitimate but slightly mis‑measured triplets, especially in the moderate‑pₜ regime, while the 3σ top‑mass veto and the loose‑W‑mass window removed a non‑trivial fraction of genuine tops; to recover the lost efficiency we should soften the non‑linear exponents (e.g. testing 0.9–1.1), replace the hard 3σ veto with a smoothly falling likelihood term, retune the σ_T(pₜ)=12+0.03·pₜ parameter using data‑driven resolution studies, and explore a more flexible pₜ‑dependent BDT weighting (perhaps a continuous function rather than a binary exponent) together with additional discriminants such as b‑tag scores or jet‑substructure observables, while performing a global hyper‑parameter optimisation (e.g. Bayesian optimisation) on an independent validation set to ensure that improvements at high pₜ do not come at the expense of overall performance.
