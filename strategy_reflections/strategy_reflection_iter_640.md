# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 640 Report

The improvement seen with strategy_v640 stems from replacing hard, binary cuts with smooth, physics‑motivated penalty terms that preserve genuine top candidates while still down‑weighting outliers: Gaussian kernels around the W‑mass and top‑mass keep signal efficiency high despite detector smearing, the pt‑factor gently rewards high‑pT triplets where reconstruction is most reliable, and the asymmetry veto suppresses ambiguous jet‑pairings that dominate combinatorial background; raising the raw BDT score to a power (α>1) restores its discriminating power that was previously damped, together yielding an efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.015, a clear gain over earlier hard‑cut approaches. However, the gains are modest and the background rejection is still limited, likely because the kernel widths, the α exponent, and the pt‑scaling were chosen heuristically rather than optimally, potentially leading to under‑ or over‑penalisation in certain kinematic regimes. The next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (σW, σtop, α, pt‑exponent) guided by a cross‑validated figure of merit such as ROC‑AUC or expected significance, possibly augmenting the score with additional discriminants (e.g. ΔR between jets, b‑tag scores) or replacing the hand‑crafted product with a learned combination via a meta‑learner (e.g. gradient‑boosted trees or a shallow neural net); finally, rigorous validation on independent control regions and assessment of systematic uncertainties will be essential before deploying the refined algorithm in an analysis.
