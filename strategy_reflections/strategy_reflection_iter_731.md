# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 731 Report

The modest yet clear gain to ε ≈ 0.62 ± 0.02 shows that the three‑pronged redesign of strategy_v731 succeeded in rescuing the semi‑boosted region without catastrophically raising the fake rate: the Gaussian top‑mass weight with a slower σ(pₜ) growth smooths out the harsh turn‑on that previously killed candidates near 150–190 GeV, the χ²‑based W‑mass consistency plus a spread‑penalty efficiently discards badly mis‑reconstructed triplets while preserving the bulk of well‑behaved ones, and the pₜ‑dependent exponent on the raw BDT score (augmented by a gentle logistic “rescue” factor) restores discrimination at high pₜ where the earlier aggressive pₜ‑turn‑on had over‑suppressed signal. The multiplicative combination forces candidates to be simultaneously good in mass, kinematics and BDT quality, which explains the recovered efficiency; however, the residual loss of ∼38 % of tops suggests that the σ scaling may still be too aggressive for the upper‑pₜ tail, the χ² penalty could be over‑penalising legitimate W‑mass fluctuations, and the rescue logistic may be admitting a small excess of background. The next steps are therefore to (i) perform a finer scan of the σ(pₜ) functional form (e.g. a piecewise linear or a saturating exponential) to flatten the high‑pₜ degradation, (ii) re‑tune the χ²‑penalty weight and the allowable spread threshold using a data‑driven W‑mass control region to minimise signal loss while keeping background rejection stable, (iii) optimise the exponent and logistic parameters (turn‑on point, steepness, rescue amplitude) with a hyper‑parameter search (grid or Bayesian) to balance high‑pₜ efficiency against fake‑rate inflation, (iv) validate the updated algorithm on independent MC generators and on pile‑up‑rich data control samples to assess robustness, and (v) explore adding complementary sub‑structure observables or a shallow neural‑network layer on top of the BDT to capture residual correlations that the current score may miss. By iterating on these knobs and quantifying both efficiency and background rejection across the full pₜ spectrum, we can aim to push the working point well above 0.65 while retaining a low mis‑tag rate.
