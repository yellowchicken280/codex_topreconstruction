# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 610 Report

The modest rise to a 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the three physics‑driven weights in strategy_v650 do succeed in pulling a fraction of otherwise‑rejected tops back into the signal sample—particularly the pT‑dependent sigmoid, which softens the harsh exponential cut for low‑pT candidates, and the χ²‑like resolution term, which rewards jets that sit within a realistic pT‑dependent mass window—but the multiplicative design still imposes an almost binary veto when any single term falls short, so marginally mis‑measured tops are still being discarded; the sigmoid also over‑boosts very soft jets, inviting more background, the resolution functions were built from simplistic Gaussian widths that fail to capture the asymmetric tails introduced by final‑state radiation and detector non‑linearities, and the dijet‑mass‑asymmetry factor, while powerful for perfectly balanced three‑jet systems, punishes genuine tops whose jets are smeared by pile‑up or out‑of‑cone energy loss, thereby eroding the net gain; to push beyond the 64 % barrier the next iteration should move away from fixed analytic forms toward learnable calibrations—derive pT‑dependent resolution parameters from data‑driven templates, re‑tune the sigmoid steepness, and either add the asymmetry term rather than multiply it or feed all three new observables together with the raw BDT score into a second‑stage classifier (e.g. a shallow neural net or a meta‑BDT) that can weigh them optimally; simultaneously, a systematic hyper‑parameter scan of the tanh scaling, the exponent of each weight, and alternative penalty shapes (e.g. Gaussian or spline smoothing) will help avoid the hard‑cut effect that still suppresses marginal tops, while cross‑validation across pT, η, and pile‑up bins will ensure the improvement is robust before a production‑level tagger is adopted.
