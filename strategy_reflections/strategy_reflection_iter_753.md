# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 753 Report

The modest 61.6 % efficiency (≈ 2.4 % below the 64 % goal and about 1.5 σ in the quoted uncertainty) tells us that the new ingredients moved the needle in the right direction but the overall selection is still too restrictive—most likely because the four multiplicative factors together create a hard “gate” that discards candidates even when only one term is marginally off, and the pₜ‑dependent calibrations were not yet optimally tuned: the logarithmically growing Gaussian width for the W‑mass term improves resolution at moderate boost but appears to over‑smear beyond ≈ 350 GeV, the 70 %–30 % best‑pair/average weighting favours the top‑pair but may under‑represent genuine ultra‑boosted configurations that still benefit from the average, the Cauchy‑like top‑mass pull, while smoother than a hard 30 GeV window, still penalises tails that contain many high‑pₜ tops, and the logistic outlier factor, with its steep fall‑off past 400 GeV, suppresses a non‑negligible fraction of signal where combinatorial background is indeed higher but not dominant. The next step is to relax the strict multiplicative architecture—e.g. replace it with a soft‑weighted sum or a calibrated likelihood—while re‑optimising the pₜ‑scaling parameters: lower the Gaussian‑width growth exponent, shift the Cauchy width scaling to a gentler rise, and flatten the logistic slope (or raise its midpoint) so that candidates above 400 GeV are only mildly down‑weighted. Additionally, scanning the best‑pair bias (testing 60 %/40 % or even a dynamic weight based on the χ² of each pairing) and validating these tweaks on an independent high‑pₜ sample will help capture the ultra‑boosted tops that the current design still loses, ultimately pushing the efficiency past the 64 % threshold.
