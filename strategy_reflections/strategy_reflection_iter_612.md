# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 612 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.015 – just shy of the 0.64 target – suggests that the physics‑driven ingredients did improve the baseline (the nearest‑W mass weight and the high‑pT logistic term clearly rescued many well‑behaved boosted tops), yet the fully multiplicative form over‑penalised a sizable fraction of otherwise good candidates, especially when the asymmetry factor or the narrow top‑mass Gaussian dragged the product below the hard cut; the aggressive BDT exponent (1.2) amplified both signal and noise, and the soft‑rescue clause was too conservative (requiring BDT > 0.6 and a broader Gaussian that still left many tail‑heavy events out).  A sensible next step is a systematic ablation and hyper‑parameter scan: loosen the top‑mass σ (or make the Gaussian width pT‑dependent), reduce the BDT exponent toward 1.0 or replace the sigmoid‑plus‑exponentiation with a calibrated isotonic mapping, and temper the asymmetry penalty (e.g. by raising its exponent or widening its tolerance).  Moreover, swapping the strict product for a weighted‐sum or a learned combination (e.g. a shallow neural net that ingests the same physics features) could preserve the complementary information without the harsh “zero‑out” when any factor is small.  Running these variations on a held‑out validation set, monitoring both tagging efficiency and the background‑rejection ROC, will pinpoint which tweak yields the missing ≈ 3 % uplift while keeping systematic stability under detector smearing.
