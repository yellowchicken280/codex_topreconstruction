# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 737 Report

The modest gain achieved by strategy_BX_v001 – lifting the integrated efficiency from the earlier flat‑plateau around 0.58 to 0.616 ± 0.015 – confirms that replacing hard, binary vetoes with smooth physics‑driven penalties does recover many genuine tops in the 150–350 GeV pT window, but the gain is still shy of the 0.64 target because the new regularisation terms are either too conservative for moderate‑pT tops or too aggressive on background, resulting in a net loss of signal acceptance that offsets the improved reconstruction; the pT‑dependent σ floor (≈12 GeV) still over‑constrains the top‑mass peak for mids, the logistic gate’s steepness suppresses a non‑trivial fraction of correctly reconstructed masses, and the pT‑scaled exponent on the raw BDT score inadvertently amplifies high‑pT signal at the expense of the mid‑range bulk, while the chi‑square consistency term and spread penalty may be double‑counting the same mismatches, diluting the overall discriminant power. To push the efficiency past 0.64 we should (i) perform a systematic hyper‑parameter scan of the σ(pT) functional form (allowing a lower floor or a gentler rise), (ii) tune the logistic gate’s midpoint and slope (or replace it with a tanh‑type soft‑cut with a configurable width), (iii) recalibrate the pT‑dependent BDT exponent so that the boost factor is modest (e.g. α(pT)=1+β·log(pT/200 GeV) with β∼0.1–0.15), (iv) re‑weight or even drop the chi‑square term if it is found redundant through an ablation study, (v) introduce additional discriminating observables such as subjet b‑tag scores, N‑subjettiness ratios or angular distances to better separate signal from combinatorial background, and (vi) employ a cross‑validated grid search (or Bayesian optimisation) on a representative validation set to jointly optimise all smoothing parameters, ensuring that the smooth penalties improve acceptance without sacrificing background rejection. Iterating with these refinements should tighten the mid‑pT top‑mass peak, preserve the gains from the smooth weighting, and finally cross the desired efficiency threshold.
