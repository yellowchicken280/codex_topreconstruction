# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 598 Report

Strategy v598 managed to lift the efficiency to 0.616 ± 0.015 by softening the overly rigid χ²‑like mass penalty and the aggressive high‑pT weighting of the previous version: the resolution‑aware Gaussian likelihood for the three‑jet mass rescued genuine tops that were previously down‑weighted by detector smearing, the smooth Gaussian on the smallest |mᵢⱼ‑m_W| gave a robust W‑pair identification without a hard cut, the tanh p_T weight provided a saturating, low‑p_T‑friendly scaling, and the logistic‑softened BDT output reduced sensitivity to statistical noise while still exploiting its discriminating power. Nevertheless, the final product of these four independently calibrated factors still assumes factor independence—a condition violated in practice because the triplet‑mass, W‑pair‑mass, BDT score and p_T are correlated—so the multiplicative form under‑estimates the true signal probability in regions where two or more variables jointly favour a candidate, keeping the overall efficiency below the 0.64 target. Moreover, using a single global σ for the mass‑likelihood ignores event‑by‑event jet‑energy‑resolution variations, and the tanh weighting may still suppress moderate‑p_T tops that contribute substantially to the sample. The next step should therefore be to replace the fixed exponents and simple product with a data‑driven multivariate combiner (e.g. a shallow boosted‑tree or a lightweight neural network) that can learn the optimal non‑linear interplay of the four ingredients while ingesting per‑event resolution estimates; concurrently we can refine the Gaussian parameters with per‑event σ, explore alternative p_T‑weight shapes (e.g. a plateaued sigmoid) and re‑tune the lightweight veto to avoid discarding borderline candidates. A systematic cross‑validation on larger simulated datasets, followed by a control‑region calibration in data, will reveal whether these refinements can finally push the efficiency above the 64 % barrier without inflating background contamination.
