# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 715 Report

The modest gain to an efficiency of 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the three “softening” innovations in strategy_v715 did indeed recover some of the moderate‑pT tail that the hard‑cut predecessor was discarding, but the net effect was still insufficient to breach the 0.64 target, suggesting that the trade‑off between background rejection and signal recovery was still not optimal; in particular the logarithmic growth of σ_top(pT) and σ_W(pT) appears to over‑inflate the mass‑resolution at high boost, diluting the joint Gaussian likelihood, while the 1.2 exponent on the BDT score may be over‑emphasising only the highest‑quality candidates and suppressing those that would benefit most from the sigmoid pT envelope, and the soft veto, though gentler, may still be penalising marginally out‑of‑range triplets too harshly. To move forward, a systematic re‑tuning of the resolution functions (e.g., a piecewise or power‑law dependence rather than pure logarithmic), a re‑balancing of the exponent weights (perhaps lowering the BDT exponent to ≈1.0 and raising the likelihood exponent to ≈1.0 or higher), and a steeper yet still smooth sigmoid transition for the pT weight should be explored; additionally, augmenting the feature set with substructure variables such as τ₃/τ₂ or energy‑correlation functions, and testing more flexible classifiers (e.g., a shallow neural net or a boosted ensemble with hyper‑parameter optimisation) could provide extra discriminating power, while cross‑validating performance in distinct pT bins will ensure that gains are not confined to a narrow region of the spectrum.
