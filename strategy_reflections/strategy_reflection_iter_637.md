# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 637 Report

The modest gain of strategy_v637 stems from the fact that its global‐consistency machinery – the geometric‑mean of the three W‑likelihoods, the chi‑square, asymmetry, spread, balance and top‑mass Gaussian terms – succeeds in down‑weighting most random dijet pairings, thereby shrinking the combinatorial background that plagued earlier selections; the re‑centred, broadened pT logistic and the soft two‑stage veto further rescue genuine low‑pT tops that would otherwise be discarded, which together push the efficiency to 61.6 % and close the gap to the 64 % target. However, the very stringency of the global constraints also begins to penalise legitimate signal events that naturally exhibit modest mass asymmetries, larger spread due to jet energy resolution, or slight deviations in the top‑to‑W mass ratio, leading to a ceiling in performance; the fixed exponents (1/9‑th power) and the sharp exponential veto, while smooth, still impose a quasi‑hard cut that can over‑suppress marginally mis‑measured tops. The next step should therefore be a targeted re‑tuning of the weight parametrisation – e.g. relaxing the asymmetry and spread penalties, allowing a tunable exponent for the geometric mean, and softening the veto curvature – guided by a systematic hyper‑parameter scan (grid or Bayesian optimisation) on a validation sample that includes realistic JES/JER variations and pile‑up. In parallel, augmenting the current physics‑driven score with a lightweight multivariate or neural‑network meta‑classifier that ingests the nine individual weights (plus b‑tag information and angular variables) could capture residual correlations and recover lost signal efficiency, while preserving the interpretability of the underlying discriminants. This two‑pronged approach—fine‑grained optimisation of the analytical weights and a data‑driven refinement layer—should help break the 64 % barrier without sacrificing background rejection.
