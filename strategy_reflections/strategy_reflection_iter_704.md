# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 704 Report

Overall, the “strategy_typeB_v705” managed to lift the signal efficiency to 0.616 ± 0.0152—a modest gain over the previous ceiling, but still short of the 0.65 target, indicating that the physics‑driven refinements helped but did not fully translate into the expected performance boost. The double‑Gaussian mixture succeeded in sharpening the W‑mass core and preserving a realistic tail, which reduced the dilution seen with the simple geometric mean; however, the tail component may still be under‑weighted for heavily mis‑measured candidates, limiting gains for the most challenging events. The pₜ‑dependent resolution improved acceptance for boosted tops, yet the static scaling law we adopted appears to over‑constrain lower‑pₜ candidates, subtly penalising them and pulling the overall efficiency down. Mapping the raw BDT score through a steep sigmoid forced the classifier into saturation for high‑score events, dwarfing complementary likelihood terms and reducing the intended multiplicative synergy. The soft asymmetry factor and gentle pₜ‑boost penalties indeed softened binary vetoes, but their coefficients were likely too modest to meaningfully influence the final probability. Going forward, we should re‑optimize the mixture weights and the Gaussian width‑vs‑pₜ functional form (perhaps using a piecewise or spline‑based scaling), tune the sigmoid’s steepness or replace it with a calibrated logistic or isotonic mapping, and revisit the exponent values in the multiplicative combination to balance the dominance of any single term. A systematic hyper‑parameter scan on an independent validation set, together with the inclusion of additional discriminating variables (e.g., angular correlations or sub‑jet grooming metrics), will help us determine whether these adjustments can push the efficiency past the 0.65 goal while preserving background rejection.
