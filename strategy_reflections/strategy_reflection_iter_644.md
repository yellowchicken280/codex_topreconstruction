# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 644 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency of strategy_B_65 shows that the smooth, p_T‑dependent penalties and the “best‑W‑match’’ idea indeed rescued a fraction of low‑p_T tops that were lost under the hard 30–130 GeV window, confirming that replacing binary vetoes with continuous kernels is a fruitful direction; however, the gain was insufficient to push us past the 0.64 goal because the Cauchy‑like top‑mass kernel shrinks too aggressively with p_T, over‑constraining high‑p_T candidates where the detector resolution is already good, and the asymmetry penalty—while effective at suppressing combinatorial background—still penalises genuine tops with modest mass spread, especially in events with imperfect jet energy corrections; additionally, the logistic p_T boost is too gentle to compensate for the tighter mass requirement at moderate p_T. The next step should therefore focus on re‑optimising the p_T‑dependent scale of the top‑mass kernel (perhaps using a milder power‑law or an empirical resolution map) and loosening the asymmetry exponent in the geometric mean so that genuine mass‑spread fluctuations are tolerated; simultaneously, we could explore a data‑driven calibration of the logistic boost amplitude and consider a secondary weighting based on per‑event jet‑energy‑resolution estimates or b‑tagging scores. A short grid‑scan of these hyper‑parameters, followed by a validation on a mixed signal–background sample, will tell whether the smoother penalties can finally cross the 0.64 efficiency threshold without sacrificing background rejection.
