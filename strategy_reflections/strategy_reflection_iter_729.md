# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 729 Report

The modest jump to a 61.6 % tagging efficiency shows that the smoother logistic pT scaling and the continuous Gaussian mass penalties succeeded in rescuing a sizable slice of semi‑boosted, low‑pT tops that were brutally cut away by the previous steep pT turn‑on and binary mass windows, while the slightly stronger BDT compression (exponent 0.55) kept the raw discriminant from dominating the score but still tamed outliers; the rescue boost then gave a final safety net for candidates already satisfying both the W‑mass and top‑mass windows, which explains the bulk of the gain.  However, the gain is still limited by residual over‑veto at intermediate pT (the logistic still penalises below ≈200 GeV) and by the fact that the Gaussian weights, while more forgiving, do not yet optimise the signal‑to‑background balance—the background mistag rate has risen roughly in step with the efficiency, indicating that the exponents in the multiplicative combination are still too permissive.  The next step should be a systematic ablation study: scan the logistic midpoint and width, vary the σ of the W‑ and top‑mass Gaussians, and fine‑tune the BDT compression exponent (perhaps in the 0.45–0.55 range) to re‑balance signal acceptance against background rejection; simultaneous optimisation of the rescue‑boost factor (or making it conditional on a background‑sensitive variable) could further tighten the tail.  Parallel to this, exploring alternative, asymmetric mass‑weight shapes (e.g. Crystal‑Ball or skewed‑Gaussian) and adding sub‑structure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy correlation functions) into the BDT could provide orthogonal discrimination power.  Finally, cross‑validating these variations on a dedicated control region and quantifying the impact on the overall ROC curve will reveal whether the current approach is merely a local optimum or if a more radical redesign—such as a shallow neural‑network tagger that learns the pT‑dependent mass correlations directly—might be warranted.
