# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 747 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency of strategy_v747 shows that the three targeted upgrades – a pₜ‑dependent resolution model, a tanh‑scaled asymmetry penalty, and a multiplicative blend of BDT score with kinematic consistency – collectively sharpened the top‑candidate selection, especially at high pₜ where the tighter Gaussian pulls suppressed the bulk of QCD combinatorics while still retaining genuine tops; the early‑veto filter cull‑ed the most egregiously inconsistent triplets before they could dilute the BDT, and the non‑linear combination allowed a strong BDT to rescue modestly mis‑measured jets, recovering the soft‑top efficiency that the previous flat ±15 GeV window had sacrificed.  Nonetheless, the gain is modest and the low‑pₜ tail still admits background, indicating that the tanh scaling may be too permissive for moderate asymmetries and that the sigma functions, though pₜ‑dependent, could be further tuned to the actual detector response (e.g. using data‑driven resolution fits).  The next step is to iterate on the sigma parametrisation and the tanh “sharpness” hyper‑parameter with a grid search on a validation set, explore a power‑law or learned weighting for the BDT‑kinematic product (or replace it with a shallow neural net that can capture more complex correlations), and finally validate the revised cuts on an independent control region to ensure that the background leakage at low pₜ is truly mitigated without sacrificing the recovered soft‑top efficiency.
