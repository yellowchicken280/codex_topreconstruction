# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 667 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the physics‑driven multiplicative dressing of the raw BDT did rescue a slice of signal that a plain “max‑pull” or hard‑window cut would have lost—most notably the joint‑W‑mass consistency term, which rewards events where all three dijet masses jointly hover around 80 GeV, and the pₜ‑logistic gate, which smooth‑ly lifts high‑pₜ tops—yet the aggressive interplay of the asymmetry penalty, the steep exp(−χ²) factor, and the hard veto (χ² > 9 or any dijet > 130 GeV) appears to have over‑suppressed legitimate signal fluctuations, especially in the resolution tails, and effectively double‑counts correlated information, dragging the overall efficiency below the 0.65 goal; the next step is therefore to re‑optimise the combination strategy by (i) treating the individual physics weights additively in log‑likelihood space rather than as a product, (ii) loosening or retuning the χ² and mass‑veto thresholds (perhaps using a soft‐clip instead of a hard cut), (iii) fine‑tuning the logistic centre and slope to match the actual pₜ spectrum in data, and (iv) folding the same non‑linear observables directly into a new BDT or a shallow neural net so that the multivariate can learn the optimal correlations, followed by a systematic cross‑validation on independent control regions to ensure that the gains persist beyond statistical fluctuations.
