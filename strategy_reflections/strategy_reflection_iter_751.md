# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 751 Report

The modest gain to 61.6 % shows that the physics‑driven smoothing and separation terms succeeded in rescuing many tops whose W‑mass dijet is smeared, confirming that a soft Gaussian weight (w_weight) and the sep_factor “near‑far” reward are effective at mitigating the hard 30 GeV veto, yet the overall target of ≈65 % was missed because the non‑linear blending still over‑penalises the tails: the 0.5 exponents on the Gaussian weights dilute the BDT’s discriminating power, the 0.75 outlier factor for w_min > 35 GeV continues to shave off a sizeable fraction of genuine high‑pT tops, and the sigmoid pt_gate may be too gentle to compensate for the resolution loss in the ultra‑boosted regime. Moreover, the fixed Gaussian widths (σ_W, σ_top) were likely too narrow for the broadened mass distributions at the highest pT, causing the top_weight to drop excessively and limiting the benefit of the sep_factor. The next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan: broaden the σ values, raise the exponent on the BDT term (e.g. 0.8–0.9) or introduce a separate scaling for high‑pT jets, replace the hard 0.75 multiplier with a smooth down‑weighting function (e.g. a logistic taper), and re‑optimize the pt_gate shape (steeper turn‑on or pT‑dependent scaling). Complementary refinements—adding jet‑substructure variables such as N‑subjettiness ratios, training a shallow neural network on the combined physics‑driven features, and validating on a dedicated high‑pT sample—should further lift the efficiency while preserving QCD background rejection.
