# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 611 Report

The modest jump to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the three‑pronged redesign—replacing the binary veto with smooth logistic/Gaussian fall‑offs, inserting a nearest‑W weight and an asymmetry penalty, and marrying the BDT score to the physics weights through tuned exponents while adding a soft‑rescue top‑mass Gaussian—indeed made the tagger more tolerant of imperfect measurements and gave a measurable boost over the purely multiplicative baseline, but the gain fell short of the 0.64‑efficiency barrier because the added penalties still dominate in borderline events and the rescue term, while preventing total loss, injects a low‑signal tail that erodes purity; in other words the new non‑linear combination is not yet optimally balanced. The next step should be a systematic hyper‑parameter scan of the logistic and Gaussian widths, the exponent values, and the rescue‑threshold, possibly via a Bayesian optimizer, while also probing alternative physics‑informed features (e.g. b‑tag probabilities, ΔR separations, helicity angles) and richer ML models (deep BDTs or lightweight neural nets) that can learn the optimal weighting from data rather than imposing fixed functional forms. Cross‑validation on a larger, more varied dataset, together with a simultaneous study of background rejection, will clarify whether the current architecture is fundamentally limited or simply under‑tuned, guiding whether incremental re‑weighting or a more radical model redesign is the most promising path forward.
