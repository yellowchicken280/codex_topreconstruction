# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 739 Report

The modest 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency shows that the pₜ‑dependent Gaussian smearing and asymmetry‑penalty ideas were indeed beneficial—the BDT retained more low‑pₜ tops than the rigid χ² cut, and the two‑stage veto prevented the wholesale loss of borderline candidates; however, the gain was insufficient to cross the 64 % target, likely because the resolution widths were still too narrow for the poorest‑resolved jets, the sigmoid asymmetry term over‑suppressed legitimate but slightly unbalanced triplets, and the final veto thresholds (|Δm|>3σ, |Δm₍top₎|>2.5σ) remained overly aggressive for the tails of the signal distribution. To push the efficiency higher we should (1) re‑tune the pₜ‑dependent σ parameters using data‑driven jet‑mass resolutions, (2) explore a softer asymmetry‐penalty (e.g. a shallower sigmoid or a piecewise linear term) that preserves signal‑like mass spreads, (3) relax the veto cuts incrementally and study the resulting background rise, (4) augment the feature set with b‑tag scores and angular variables that further separate true tops from combinatorial backgrounds, and (5) run a systematic hyper‑parameter sweep (including alternative BDT depths, learning rates, or even a shallow neural net) on an independent validation set to locate the optimal balance between signal acceptance and background rejection before committing to a final configuration.
