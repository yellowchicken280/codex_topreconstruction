# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 748 Report

The 0.616 ± 0.015 efficiency achieved by strategy_v748 shows that the non‑linear blend of a pₜ‑dependent resolution model, Gaussian‑like χ² mass pulls, an early 5σ dijet‑mass veto, a sigmoid‑scaled BDT score and a modest additive kinematic weight does succeed in tightening kinematic consistency and suppressing the bulk of low‑pₜ QCD combinatorics without catastrophically sacrificing genuine top candidates, especially at high pₜ where the narrowed resolution windows are most effective; however, the relatively modest signal efficiency suggests that the veto and the tight χ² windows also excise a non‑negligible fraction of real tops that experience radiation or detector fluctuations that push one of the pulls just beyond the chosen thresholds, and the sigmoid saturation may be damping useful discriminating power from the highest‑scoring BDT candidates.  Going forward, a sensible next step is to re‑optimize the early‑veto sigma (e.g. testing 4σ or a pₜ‑dependent cut), adjust the χ² weighting (perhaps scaling the dijet‑mass pull term to be less punitive for modest outliers), and fine‑tune the sigmoid’s slope and saturation point to retain more of the genuinely high‑signal events while still limiting outlier dominance; meanwhile, incorporating additional substructure observables (N‑subjettiness, energy‑correlation functions) or a small neural‑network layer that can learn a more flexible pₜ‑dependent resolution could further improve background rejection, and systematic cross‑validation on independent samples (including varying pile‑up conditions) will be essential to confirm robustness before deploying the refined version.
