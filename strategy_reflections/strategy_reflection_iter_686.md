# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 686 Report

The modest jump to a 61.6 % signal efficiency shows that softening the rigid 45 GeV W‑mass veto and turning the three dijet mass cuts into smooth, pₜ‑dependent Gaussian quality scores successfully rescued a sizable fraction of genuine tops that were being discarded by the hard window, especially in the boosted regime where jet‑energy smearing inflates the reconstructed dijet masses; the parallel Gaussian on the triplet mass further rewards overall top‑like kinematics without catastrophically penalising a single outlier, and the gentle sigmoid pₜ turn‑on preserves low‑pₜ acceptance while modestly up‑weighting the more reliable high‑pₜ candidates, all of which combine to give a higher acceptance without a dramatic drop in raw BDT discriminant.  Nevertheless, the current metric only tracks efficiency – we have yet to quantify any accompanying loss in background rejection or overall purity, and the fixed choices of half‑width (30 GeV + 0.05 pₜ), σ≈30 GeV for the top‑mass term, and the product‑based combination may not be optimal across the full pₜ spectrum or for different detector resolutions.  The next steps should therefore include a systematic study of the receiver‑operating‑characteristic (ROC) curve to assess the trade‑off between efficiency and background rejection, a parameter scan (or even a data‑driven fit) to tune the half‑width slope and Gaussian widths, an exploration of alternative non‑linear aggregations (e.g. a log‑sum‑exp or a small neural‑network wrapper) that could automatically balance the three dijet scores against the triplet mass, and finally a cross‑validation on an independent simulated sample (and eventually real data control regions) to ensure the improvement is robust against variations in jet‑energy resolution, pile‑up conditions, and systematic uncertainties.
