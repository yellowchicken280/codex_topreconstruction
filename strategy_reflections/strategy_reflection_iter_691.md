# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 691 Report

The modest gain to 0.616 ± 0.0152 shows that the smooth, physics‑inspired ingredients of strategy_nova_65 do indeed rescue a fraction of borderline candidates—most notably the sigmoid pT weight, which rescued many 300–500 GeV triplets that were lost to the hard cuts of the previous iteration—but the hand‑crafted multiplicative form still over‑penalises a sizable share of genuine tops: the variance‑based spread penalty and the “uniqueness” term tend to suppress events with naturally broader dijet mass distributions (especially at higher pT where jet resolution degrades), and the Gaussian factors, set with relatively narrow σ around the world‑average top and W masses, continue to clip tops that are shifted by detector effects; consequently the two‑stage veto, while looser on the overall top‑mass window, still eliminates a non‑negligible tail of correctly reconstructed tops. The next step is therefore a systematic hyper‑parameter scan—broadening the Gaussian σ, softening the exponent on the spread penalty, and recalibrating the uniqueness weight—coupled with a data‑driven optimisation (e.g. Bayesian optimisation or grid search on a validation set) to find the optimal balance between signal efficiency and background rejection. Additionally, it would be worthwhile to replace the purely multiplicative architecture with a learned combination (e.g. a shallow logistic‑regression or a small neural net) that can adaptively weight each ingredient per‑event, and to explore separate pT regimes with tailored parameter sets so that high‑pT topologies are not unfairly penalised. By iterating along these lines the efficiency should be able to cross the 0.65 target without sacrificing the improved background discrimination that the smooth functions already provide.
