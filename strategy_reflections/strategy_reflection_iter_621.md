# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 621 Report

Strategy v621 proved that a more nuanced treatment of the three dijet masses can lift the top‑tag efficiency—from a baseline of roughly 60 % to 61.6 % ± 1.5 %—by preserving the genuine W‑decay (through the dW_best extraction) and softening the hard Gaussian cuts with exponential weights, while the asymmetry‑based sigmoid and the pT‑dependent tanh scaling helped retain boosted tops that would otherwise be rejected by static mass windows. Nevertheless, the residual shortfall relative to the 64 % goal indicates that the current penalty functions are still too aggressive for a non‑trivial fraction of real tops, especially those suffering moderate ISR that trigger the asymmetry sigmoid or fall victim to the three lightweight vetoes. The fixed 15 GeV turn‑on of the sigmoid and the static exponential slopes do not fully track the pT‑evolution of radiation, leading to a lingering loss of medium‑quality, high‑pT candidates. The next iteration should therefore make the asymmetry cut pT‑dependent (e.g., a sliding threshold or a gentler sigmoid at large pT), relax or replace the vetoes with a learned classifier that can better discriminate combinatorial tails, and enrich the input set with jet‑substructure observables (τ_32, ECF ratios, groomed masses) that recover tops with hard radiation. Embedding these variables into a second‑stage BDT or a lightweight graph‑neural network will allow the algorithm to learn optimal non‑linear weightings rather than relying on hand‑crafted exponentials, while systematic cross‑validation on multiple MC tunes will guard against over‑tuning; these refinements should close the remaining 2–3 % gap and bring the efficiency up to the 64 % target without compromising background rejection.
