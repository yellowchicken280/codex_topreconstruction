# Top Quark Reconstruction - Iteration 655 Report

The 61.6 %±1.5 % efficiency of strategy_v655 shows that the three‑component design—dynamic Gaussian W‑likelihood, dynamic top‑likelihood, and the smooth p_T‑boost factor—does capture much of the kinematic structure of true hadronic tops, but its multiplicative weighting, aggressive hierarchy enforcement, and the hard two‑stage veto together over‑prune signal events, especially those with modest boosts or with one of the non‑W dijets dragged down by final‑state radiation or pile‑up fluctuations; the Gaussian widths shrinking too quickly with p_T and the logistic quality cut at ≈130 GeV also appear to be too punitive for a sizable tail of genuine tops, which explains why we fall short of the 64 % target despite a respectable statistical precision. Going forward, a systematic retuning of the σ(p_T) scaling and the logistic threshold (perhaps moving the quality cut down to ~115 GeV or making it p_T‑dependent) should be tried, along with a softened hierarchy factor (e.g. replacing the sharp product with a weighted sum or a soft‑max) and a broadened, p_T‑scaled mass‑window veto (or a probabilistic rather than binary veto). Adding complementary handles—b‑tag discriminants, N‑subjettiness ratios, or even a small gradient‑boosted decision tree that learns optimal combinations of the three likelihoods—could further recover lost signal while preserving background rejection. A focused grid scan of these hyper‑parameters, validated on a separate cross‑validation sample, will reveal whether the boosted‑resolution gains can finally push the efficiency above the 64 % barrier.
